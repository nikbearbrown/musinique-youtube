Maxwell's Equations Consistency Checker: Light Falls Out | Claude for Science

In 1865, Maxwell added one term to fix a mathematical contradiction in Ampère's law. From that fix, in empty space, a wave equation falls out with speed 1/√(μ₀ε₀). Plug in SI constants measured with no reference to light — and you get c.

We build maxwell_check.py with Claude: derive c from μ₀ and ε₀ via scipy.constants, then run a 1D leapfrog wave simulation. The Gaussian packet propagates at exactly c. Then we add two pulses traveling toward each other and watch them pass through unchanged — superposition, demonstrated numerically.

→ Your turn: replace the Gaussian with a square wave, evolve 1000 steps, and measure numerical dispersion. Prompt in the video.
