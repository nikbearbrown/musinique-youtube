# PEDAGOGY — mutational-signatures-exposure

## Learning objective
Students understand mutational signatures as a forensic record of mutational processes, can name five major COSMIC signatures with their exposures, and identify which signatures are clinically actionable.

## Target audience
Cancer biology students, genomics trainees, oncology clinicians learning precision medicine.

## Core concept
Every tumor accumulates mutations through specific processes — tobacco, UV, APOBEC, MMR failure, aflatoxin. Each process leaves a characteristic pattern of base changes. Reading that pattern reveals what happened to the cell historically. Of the five major signatures, SBS6 (MMR deficiency) is the most directly actionable: MSI-high tumors identified via SBS6 qualify for pembrolizumab under the tumor-agnostic approval.

## Pedagogical approach
- Never-smoker with APOBEC signature creates genuine surprise: the genome contradicts the clinical history
- Five-bar visualization makes the signatures visually distinct and memorable
- Clinical case annotation (SBS6 to pembrolizumab) shows the path from signature to treatment decision

## Key takeaway
Mutational signatures are forensic evidence, not treatment targets. They reveal what happened, not what to do — except for SBS6/MSI-high, where the signature directly triggers an eligibility test.

## Misconception addressed
Students sometimes think all mutations are equivalent. This reel shows that mutation patterns differ by exposure, and that pattern recognition is clinically meaningful specifically for the MMR-deficiency signature.
