# PEDAGOGY — brca-parp-synthetic-lethality

## Learning objective
Students understand synthetic lethality as a geometric relationship between two molecular vulnerabilities, and can explain why the BRCA/PARP paradigm succeeds in specific contexts but not universally.

## Target audience
Cancer biology students, oncology trainees, researchers in targeted therapy.

## Core concept
Synthetic lethality requires two conditions: (1) the tumor has a specific defect in one repair pathway, and (2) a drug can block a second pathway that the tumor now depends on. When either condition fails — the defect is absent, the partner is not druggable, or the tumor has alternative repair — the geometric relationship collapses.

## Pedagogical approach
- The 2x2 grid makes the logic visual and memorable
- MTAP-PRMT5 comparison shows how the concept extends beyond BRCA
- The resistance mechanism (BRCA reversion) demonstrates that even confirmed pairs can be escaped

## Key takeaway
Synthetic lethality is not a drug class. It is a relationship. Predicting which tumors will respond requires knowing whether the geometry actually holds in that patient's specific tumor.

## Misconception addressed
Students sometimes think "PARP inhibitor = any cancer with DNA damage." This reel corrects that: the drug works only when BRCA-mediated HR repair is genuinely absent, which requires testing, not assumption.
