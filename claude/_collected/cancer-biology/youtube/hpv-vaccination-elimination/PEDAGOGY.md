# PEDAGOGY — hpv-vaccination-elimination

## Learning objective
Students understand that cancer elimination is a quantitative public health target (not zero cases), know the WHO threshold, and can explain why the Australian projection depends on sustained policy rather than completed biology.

## Target audience
Cancer biology students, public health trainees, oncology policy analysts.

## Core concept
The biology of HPV-driven cervical cancer is completely understood: HPV16/18 drive ~70% of cases; the vaccine prevents infection; no infection means no HPV-driven cancer. The virology is solved. Elimination depends on whether vaccination rates are sustained across future birth cohorts for decades — this is a policy and behavioral question, not a biological one.

## Pedagogical approach
- The Australia vs. Romania comparison makes the coverage-outcome relationship concrete
- The threshold sensitivity analysis (what happens if coverage drops) shows the fragility of the projection
- WHO's operational definition of "elimination" as a measurable threshold prevents vague thinking

## Key takeaway
Cancer prevention through vaccination is a solvable problem. The limit on progress is not scientific — it is the sustained commitment to vaccination across future cohorts. Elimination is achievable and reversible.

## Misconception addressed
Students sometimes think elimination means eradication (zero cases). This reel clarifies that elimination is a public health definition with a specific numerical threshold, and that it is a population-level commitment, not a one-time achievement.
