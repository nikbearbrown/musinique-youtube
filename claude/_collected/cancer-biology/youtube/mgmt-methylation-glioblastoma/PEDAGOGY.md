# PEDAGOGY — mgmt-methylation-glioblastoma

## Learning objective
Students understand why MGMT promoter methylation predicts temozolomide response (not just prognosis), can state the survival difference from Hegi 2005, and explain the mechanistic logic: silencing the repair gene prevents repair of drug-induced DNA damage.

## Target audience
Neuro-oncology trainees, cancer biology students, clinical pharmacology students.

## Core concept
Temozolomide alkylates guanine at the O6 position, creating a lesion that can cause cell death. MGMT is a repair enzyme that reverses this specific modification. If MGMT is silenced by promoter methylation, the lesion persists and the cancer cell dies. If MGMT is active (unmethylated), it repairs the damage and the drug fails. Counterintuitively, the epigenetically silenced gene is a therapeutic asset.

## Pedagogical approach
- The counterintuitive hook is the pedagogical engine: broken repair = better outcome
- Decision tree makes the two branches and their consequences clear
- The PTEN haploinsufficiency comparison (from reel 06) shows this is part of a broader pattern

## Key takeaway
The MGMT biomarker is predictive (predicts response to a specific drug) not merely prognostic (predicts outcome regardless of treatment). This distinction is critical for clinical use: it determines whether methylation status should change the treatment plan.

## Misconception addressed
Students sometimes think that repairing DNA is always good for the patient. This reel shows that in the context of DNA-damaging chemotherapy, repair is the enemy — the drug works by creating damage the tumor cannot fix.
