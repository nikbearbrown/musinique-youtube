## Claims Verification

| Claim | Beat | Verdict | Source / Note |
|-------|------|---------|---------------|
| Cyclin D is induced by external growth-factor signals and is intrinsically unstable, degraded within minutes if the signal drops | B02, B10 | CORRECT | Chapter: "cyclin D, which is induced by external growth-factor signals and is intrinsically unstable, degraded within minutes if the signal drops" |
| Hypophosphorylated Rb holds E2F transcription factors bound at S-phase gene promoters | B02, B07 | CORRECT | Chapter: "hypophosphorylated Rb binds the E2F transcription factors and holds them at the promoters of S-phase genes, blocking transcription" |
| Cyclin D–CDK4/6 phosphorylates Rb; partial phosphorylation releases some E2F | B07 | CORRECT | Chapter: "cyclin D–CDK4/6 activity, the complex phosphorylates Rb. Partially phosphorylated Rb releases some E2F, which transcribes cyclin E" |
| Cyclin E pairs with CDK2 and further phosphorylates Rb — a positive feedback loop | B08 | CORRECT | Chapter: "Cyclin E pairs with CDK2 and further phosphorylates Rb — a positive-feedback loop" |
| Hyperphosphorylated Rb releases all E2F; full S-phase program switches on | B08, B11 | CORRECT | Chapter: "Hyperphosphorylated Rb releases all E2F, and the full S-phase program switches on: cyclin E, cyclin A, DNA polymerases" |
| Before restriction point: removing growth factors causes cyclin D to fall, Rb to become hypophosphorylated, cell to return to G0 | B10 | CORRECT | Chapter: "Before it, removing growth factors causes cyclin D to fall, Rb to become hypophosphorylated again, and the cell to return to G0" |
| After restriction point: feedback carried Rb phosphorylation to hyperphosphorylated state; E2F fully active; removing growth factors does not reverse the decision | B11 | CORRECT | Chapter: "After it, the feedback has carried Rb phosphorylation to the hyperphosphorylated state, E2F is fully active, and the S-phase program is already running — removing growth factors at this point does not reverse the decision" |
| Bistability: two stable states, sharp transition | B09 | CORRECT | Chapter: "This is bistability: two stable states, with a sharp transition between them" |
| Cyclin E is E2F-driven after restriction point, not growth-factor-driven | B11, B12 | CORRECT | Chapter implies this via the feedback loop — cyclin E transcription driven by E2F, not directly by growth factors |
| CDK4/6 pairs with cyclin D | B07 | CORRECT | Chapter: "Cyclin D + CDK4/6 drives progression through G1" |
