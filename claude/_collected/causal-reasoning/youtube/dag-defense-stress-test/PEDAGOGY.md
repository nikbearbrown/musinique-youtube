# PEDAGOGY
Concept: structured DAG defense — making assumptions visible and attackable.
Problem: how do you defend a DAG without just asserting it is correct?
Question on screen: what are the three parts of a rigorous DAG defense?
Answer: (1) mechanistic justification for each arrow, (2) ranking of dangerous missing arrows by bias direction, (3) named unmeasured confounders with bias direction. Claude drafts — you verify.
