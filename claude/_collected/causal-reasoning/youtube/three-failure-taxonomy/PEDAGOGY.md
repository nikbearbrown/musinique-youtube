# PEDAGOGY
Concept: three-failure taxonomy for DAG critique.
Problem: two LLMs propose different DAGs for the same problem — which fails worse?
Question on screen: what are the three ways a DAG can be wrong?
Answer: wrong variables (omissions), wrong arrows (direction errors), wrong conditioning (collider/mediator confusion). Conditioning on a mediator is the most dangerous — it blocks the path you are trying to estimate.
