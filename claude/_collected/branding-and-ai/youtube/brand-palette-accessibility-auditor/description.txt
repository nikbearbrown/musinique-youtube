Build a Brand Palette Accessibility Auditor with Claude — part of Nina Harris' course Branding and AI.

WCAG 2.2 AA: 4.5:1 for normal text, 3:1 for large text and graphical objects. A palette that fails this is not a stylistic choice — it is a documented barrier to access, and in many jurisdictions a legal requirement. The correction is almost always minor: darken or lighten 15-20%. Warm-clay #A89068 on white comes in at 3.1:1 — FAIL for normal text. Darken to #7A6045 and the ratio crosses 4.5. The palette character does not change. The implementation is now compliant.

Prompt used in this video:
  claude "WCAG 2.2 AA contrast audit for palette:
    [HEX CODES WITH LABELS]
    Test pairings: [TEXT HEX] on [BACKGROUND HEX]
    Relative luminance: L = 0.2126R + 0.7152G + 0.0722B
    (linearize: x/255 ≤0.04045→x/12.92, else ((x+0.055)/1.055)^2.4)
    Ratio = (L_lighter+0.05)/(L_darker+0.05)
    Rate: PASS-AAA(≥7) | PASS-AA-normal(≥4.5) |
          PASS-AA-large(≥3) | FAIL
    For FAIL: nearest hex same hue → AA + corrected ratio"
