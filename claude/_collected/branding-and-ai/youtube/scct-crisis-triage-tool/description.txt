Crisis response strategy that doesn't match the crisis type makes reputational damage worse. Situational Crisis Communication Theory (Coombs, 2007) maps three crisis types — Victim, Accidental, Preventable — to specific response strategies. Applying a Deny strategy to a Preventable crisis (like BP Deepwater Horizon) fires a STRATEGY MISMATCH WARNING. Tylenol 1982 is the canonical Victim case: 31 million bottles recalled, rebuild strategy, brand survived.

Prompt used in this video:
  claude "triage this crisis scenario:
    [INCIDENT DESCRIPTION]
    SCCT classification: VICTIM | ACCIDENTAL | PREVENTABLE
    Recommended strategies for that type.
    Draft holding statement (50 words, factual, DRAFT).
    STRATEGY MISMATCH WARNING if wrong strategy applied."

All outputs are DRAFT — professional communications counsel required before any public statement.
