Build a Greenwashing Claims Risk Checker with Claude — part of Nina Harris' course Branding and AI.

EU ECGT Directive: adopted March 2024. Applies 27 September 2026. Two specific bans: (1) generic green claims without substantiation — "eco-friendly," "green," "sustainable," "climate-smart" are banned at the product level unless you can show the evidence; (2) offset-based "climate neutral" or "net zero" at the product level. A claim you cannot substantiate is not a brand claim — it is a liability. This checker flags every [ECGT-RISK] item, assigns HAVE/NEED/CANNOT-SUBSTANTIATE status, and produces a compliant revised version for every HIGH-REGULATORY claim.

Prompt used in this video:
  claude "greenwashing risk check — ECGT + FTC:
    Claims: [LIST SUSTAINABILITY / ETHICS CLAIMS]
    Scope: PRODUCT-LEVEL or CORPORATE-LEVEL per claim
    Flag [ECGT-RISK]: generic green + offset-based climate neutral
    Risk: HIGH-REGULATORY | MEDIUM | LOW
    Status: HAVE | NEED | CANNOT-SUBSTANTIATE
    For HIGH + CANNOT-SUBSTANTIATE: revised compliant version."
