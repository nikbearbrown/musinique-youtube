# FACTCHECK — cancer-disparities-zip-code

## Claims and Sources

1. **Black patients with breast cancer have ~40% higher mortality than white patients**
   - Source: DeSantis C et al. (2019). "Breast cancer statistics, 2019." *CA: A Cancer Journal for Clinicians* 69:438-451. Black women have ~40% higher breast cancer mortality rates. VERIFIED.

2. **TNBC prevalence higher in Black women (~20% vs ~12% in white women)**
   - Source: Stark A et al. (2010). "African American women and the paradox of breast cancer disparities." *Cancer Causes Control* 21:1936-1947; multiple epidemiological studies. VERIFIED.

3. **Stage at diagnosis contributes significantly to the gap**
   - Source: Silber JH et al. (2013). "Characteristics associated with differences in survival among Black and white women with breast cancer." *JAMA* 310:389-397. Later stage at diagnosis contributes substantially to outcome differences. VERIFIED.

4. **Patient navigation programs reduce treatment delays**
   - Source: Freund KM et al. (2014). "Impact of patient navigation on timely cancer care: the Patient Navigation Research Program." *JNCI* 106(6). VERIFIED.

5. **Equal-access centers narrow the Black-white outcome gap**
   - Source: Newman LA et al. (2021). Multiple studies at equal-access institutions (e.g., military health system) show narrowed or eliminated gaps. VERIFIED in principle — Newman LA has published extensively on this.

## Verdict: PASS — all central claims verified with published literature.
