# PEDAGOGY — cancer-disparities-zip-code

## Learning Objective
Understand the causal structure of racial disparities in breast cancer outcomes — separating biological from structural contributors — and identify evidence-based interventions that can close the gap.

## Core Concept
The Black-white breast cancer mortality gap (~40%) has a complex causal structure. Biology contributes — Black women have higher TNBC prevalence, which is harder to treat. But biology cannot explain the full gap. When researchers control for stage at diagnosis and treatment quality (equal-access studies, military health system), the biological disadvantage accounts for less of the gap than structural factors.

## Causal Attribution Framework
- TNBC biology: ~25% of gap (real, addressable with better drugs)
- Later stage at diagnosis: ~30% (screening access gap — structural, addressable)
- Treatment delays: ~15% (navigation programs shown to help — structural)
- Treatment quality: ~20% (equal-access systems narrow gap — structural)
- Trial underenrollment: ~10% (policy and diversity requirements — structural)

## Key Insight
75% of the mortality gap is structural, not biological. Structural gaps are the result of policy choices — screening program funding, hospital location, insurance coverage, trial design. They can be fixed by policy decisions. Biology requires R&D. Structural equity requires will.

## Pedagogical Structure
- Establish the gap is real (40% higher mortality)
- Decompose biology vs. structure with evidence
- Show each structural factor has a proven intervention
- Frame: most of the gap is addressable right now with existing tools
