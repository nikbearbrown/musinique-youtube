# PEDAGOGY — pre-metastatic-niche

## Learning Objective
Understand the pre-metastatic niche as a tumor-prepared microenvironment in distant organs, created by exosomes with organ-specific integrin addresses, before cancer cells arrive — and identify the therapeutic opportunity this represents.

## Core Concept
The tumor sends exosome "scouts" to prepare distant organs for colonization. These exosomes carry surface integrins that function as organ-specific addresses (avb5→liver, a6b4→lung). At the target organ, exosomes remodel the ECM with fibronectin, recruit immunosuppressive MDSCs, and create a pro-tumorigenic microenvironment. CTCs that arrive find a hospitable, prepared niche rather than a hostile foreign tissue.

## Historical Milestones
- Kaplan 2005 (Nature): VEGFR1+ bone marrow progenitors pre-home to metastatic sites — defining the concept
- Hoshino 2015 (Nature): Integrin-directed exosome homing established organ-specific tropism
- Peinado 2012 (Nature Medicine): Melanoma exosomes educate bone marrow progenitors

## Therapeutic Logic
If niche formation is a prerequisite for successful metastasis, blocking niche formation prevents colonization regardless of how many CTCs are circulating. This is a preventive anti-metastatic strategy — not treating existing metastases, but preventing new ones from forming.

## Clinical Challenge
Translating niche-blocking to patients requires:
1. Detecting active niche formation (exosome detection in blood — in development)
2. Knowing which organ is being prepared (integrin profiling of blood exosomes)
3. A safe integrin-blocking antibody (preclinical stage)
