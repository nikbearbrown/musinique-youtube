# PEDAGOGY — tumor-heterogeneity-tracerx

## Learning Objective
Understand intratumoral heterogeneity as a clinical problem revealed by TRACERx, and grasp why clonal (trunk) mutations are superior therapeutic and immunotherapy targets compared to subclonal mutations.

## Core Concept
A tumor is not a uniform mass of identical cells. It is a population of genetically distinct subclones — each descended from the original cancer cell but carrying additional private mutations. The clonal architecture has a trunk (mutations present in all cells, fixed early) and branches (mutations present in subsets, accumulated later).

## TRACERx Key Finding
Multi-region sequencing revealed that many driver mutations once thought to be "clonal" (present in all cells) were actually subclonal — present in only a fraction of the tumor. A single biopsy, by sampling only one region, cannot capture this diversity. This means treatment plans built on single-biopsy mutation profiles may be targeting a minority of the tumor.

## Clinical Implication: Target the Trunk
1. Trunk mutations are present in 100% of cells → therapy or immunotherapy targets every cell
2. Subclonal mutations are present in a fraction → therapy misses cells lacking the target
3. Clonal neoantigen burden (McGranahan 2016) predicts immunotherapy response
4. High clonal neoantigens = better IO response = trunk targeting works

## Sampling Solutions
- Multi-region biopsy: most complete, but invasive and impractical
- ctDNA: captures all circulating tumor DNA from all subclones (weighted by cell number)
- Single-cell sequencing: research tool, not yet clinical
