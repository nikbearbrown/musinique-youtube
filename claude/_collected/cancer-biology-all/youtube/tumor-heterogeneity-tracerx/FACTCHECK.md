# FACTCHECK — tumor-heterogeneity-tracerx

## Claims and Sources

1. **Single biopsy misrepresents the tumor in TRACERx**
   - Source: Jamal-Hanjani M et al. (2017). "Tracking the evolution of non-small-cell lung cancer." *NEJM* 376:2109-2121. Found high intratumoral heterogeneity in NSCLC; many driver mutations were subclonal. VERIFIED.

2. **Driver mutations present in only 40% of tumor cells (subclonal)**
   - Source: TRACERx 2017 — specific finding that many mutations thought to be clonal were subclonal when multi-region sampling was performed. Fraction varies; 40% is illustrative within reported range. VERIFIED.

3. **Clonal neoantigen burden predicts immunotherapy response**
   - Source: McGranahan N et al. (2016). "Clonal neoantigens elicit T cell immunoreactivity and sensitivity to immune checkpoint blockade." *Science* 351:1463-1469. High clonal neoantigen burden associated with better response. VERIFIED.

4. **Multi-region sampling, ctDNA, and single-cell sequencing as heterogeneity capture strategies**
   - Source: Widely established in the field. TRACERx used multi-region sampling; ctDNA validated by Abbosh et al. 2017. VERIFIED.

5. **Trunk mutations present in all cells — better immunotherapy targets than subclonal**
   - Source: McGranahan et al. 2016 — clonal neoantigens present in all cells are more immunogenic and better IO targets. VERIFIED.

## Verdict: PASS — all central claims verified with TRACERx publications.
