# PEDAGOGY — neoantigen-vaccine-pipeline

## Learning Objective
Understand the personalized neoantigen vaccine pipeline — from tumor sequencing to immune response — and identify the manufacturing and access bottlenecks that prevent broad adoption.

## Core Concept
Cancer neoantigens are mutant peptides unique to a patient's tumor. By sequencing the tumor, predicting which peptides bind MHC class I/II, and synthesizing a personalized mRNA vaccine, the immune system can be trained to recognize and kill tumor cells bearing those mutations. Unlike checkpoint inhibitors (which remove brakes), vaccines add gas — active tumor-specific immunity.

## Pipeline Stages and Failure Points
1. Biopsy + QC: ~10% attrition (insufficient tumor material)
2. WGS + sequencing: ~5% attrition (quality/turnaround failures)
3. Neoantigen prediction: ~15% attrition (poor HLA binding prediction, low immunogenicity)
4. mRNA manufacturing: ~8% attrition (synthesis failures, QC release)
5. Dosing: ~2% attrition

Cumulative: ~33% of patients may not receive a dose — this is the manufacturing challenge.

## Key Evidence
- mRNA-4157 + pembrolizumab: 44% reduction in recurrence or death in resected stage III/IV melanoma (Phase 2b, KEYNOTE-942)
- Extension trials in NSCLC and colorectal: ongoing

## Scale Challenge
At ~$57,000 total cost per patient and 45-60 day manufacturing window, personalized neoantigen vaccines face severe access barriers. The field needs manufacturing automation, cost reduction, and health system infrastructure to deliver this at scale.
