# FACTCHECK — pfs-surrogate-endpoint

## Claims and Sources

1. **Bevacizumab breast cancer approval withdrawn after OS failure**
   - Source: FDA withdrew accelerated approval for bevacizumab in breast cancer in 2011 after E2100 and subsequent trials failed to show OS benefit despite PFS improvement (~5.9 months). VERIFIED.

2. **Bevacizumab glioblastoma — PFS benefit, no OS benefit, approval maintained**
   - Source: Chinot OL et al. (2014). "Bevacizumab plus radiotherapy–temozolomide for newly diagnosed glioblastoma." *NEJM* 370:709-722. PFS benefit (4.4 months), no OS benefit, approval maintained. VERIFIED.

3. **FOLFOX colorectal first-line — PFS translates to OS**
   - Source: de Gramont A et al. (2000). "Leucovorin and fluorouracil with or without oxaliplatin as first-line treatment in advanced colorectal cancer." *JCO* 18:2938-2947. First-line colorectal is one of the validated PFS→OS settings. VERIFIED.

4. **Prasad and Mailankody 2017 — proportion of accelerated approvals confirmed with OS**
   - Source: Prasad V & Mailankody S (2017). "Research and development spending to bring a single cancer drug to market and revenues after approval." *JAMA Internal Medicine* 177:1569-1575. (Related work on surrogate endpoint confirmation also by same authors). PLAUSIBLE — verify exact citation for surrogate confirmation rates.

5. **PFS-OS correlation varies by cancer type and line of therapy**
   - Source: Buyse M et al. Multiple meta-analyses showing surrogacy varies. Kim C & Prasad V (2015). *JAMA Internal Medicine*. VERIFIED.

## Verdict: PASS — central claims verified; Prasad citation should be double-checked for exact paper.
