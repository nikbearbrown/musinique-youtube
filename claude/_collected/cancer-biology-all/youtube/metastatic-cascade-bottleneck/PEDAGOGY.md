# PEDAGOGY — metastatic-cascade-bottleneck

## Learning Objective
Understand the quantitative inefficiency of metastasis and identify therapeutic opportunities at early bottleneck steps rather than the clinically visible macrometastasis stage.

## Core Concept
Metastasis is a multi-step process with massive attrition at each step. A tumor can shed 10^6 cells per day into circulation, yet only ~1 in 10^5 forms a clinically detectable metastasis. Each step represents a molecular bottleneck — a targetable vulnerability. The tragedy is that approved therapies almost exclusively target the final step (macrometastasis), when the selection battle is already over.

## Cascade Steps and Vulnerabilities
1. Intravasation: anoikis resistance (tumor cells detach from ECM and survive)
2. Circulation: NK cell killing, shear stress survival
3. Extravasation: CXCR4-CXCL12 organ homing signals
4. Micromet formation: ECM remodeling, dormancy entry/exit
5. Macrometastasis: angiogenic switch (VEGF/VEGFR)

## Therapeutic Opportunity Framing
Earlier steps in the cascade = fewer cells to target = lower drug efficacy required to shift outcomes. Dormancy maintenance (preventing micromet → macrometastasis) and anti-extravasation strategies (blocking CXCR4) represent the least crowded therapeutic space.
