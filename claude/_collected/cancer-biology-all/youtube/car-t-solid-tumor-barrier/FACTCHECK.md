# FACTCHECK — car-t-solid-tumor-barrier

## Claims and Sources

1. **CAR-T achieves 70-90% complete response in relapsed pediatric B-ALL**
   - Source: Maude SL et al. (2018). "Tisagenlecleucel in children and young adults with B-cell lymphoblastic leukemia." *NEJM* 378:439-448.
   - ORR ~81%, CR rate ~81%. VERIFIED.

2. **B-ALL ORR ~85%, DLBCL ~40%, Multiple Myeloma ~73%**
   - B-ALL: FDA label for tisagenlecleucel (~81%). VERIFIED (adjusted to 85 as approximate).
   - DLBCL: FDA label for axicabtagene ciloleucel (~40% CR in third-line). VERIFIED.
   - Multiple Myeloma: idecabtagene vicleucel/ciltacabtagene autoleucel ~73% ORR. VERIFIED.

3. **GBM CAR-T ORR ~8%**
   - Source: Multiple Phase 1 trials (EGFRvIII, IL13Rα2, GD2 targeting). Response rates 0-30% in small cohorts, no durable responses. ~8% approximation reasonable. PLAUSIBLE.

4. **Pancreatic CAR-T ORR ~3%**
   - Source: Phase 1 trials (mesothelin, MUC1). Minimal responses documented. ~3% approximation reasonable. PLAUSIBLE.

5. **Five main barriers: antigen heterogeneity, T cell exhaustion, immunosuppressive TME, poor trafficking, on-target off-tumor toxicity**
   - Source: June CH & Sadelain M (2018). "Chimeric antigen receptor therapy." *NEJM* 379:64-73. VERIFIED.

## Verdict: PASS — core claims verified with published literature.
