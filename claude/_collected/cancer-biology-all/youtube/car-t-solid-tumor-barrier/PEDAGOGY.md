# PEDAGOGY — car-t-solid-tumor-barrier

## Learning Objective
Understand why CAR-T cell therapy succeeds in liquid cancers but fails in solid tumors, and identify the five engineering barriers that must be overcome.

## Core Concept
CAR-T cells are engineered T cells that express chimeric antigen receptors targeting tumor-specific antigens. In hematologic cancers, the tumor cells are accessible in blood and lymph, express uniform target antigens, and the immune microenvironment is permissive. In solid tumors, none of these conditions hold.

## Five Barriers (each independent)
1. **Antigen heterogeneity**: Not all solid tumor cells express the CAR target — escape is inevitable
2. **T cell exhaustion**: Chronic antigen exposure in the TME drives inhibitory receptor upregulation
3. **Immunosuppressive TME**: Tregs, MDSCs, TGF-beta, and PD-L1 suppress CAR-T function
4. **Poor trafficking**: CAR-T cells cannot penetrate fibrotic stroma to reach tumor cells
5. **On-target/off-tumor toxicity**: Target antigens expressed on normal tissue limit safe dosing

## Key Insight
Each barrier is independent — solving one does not solve the others. A combination approach is required. The field is developing solutions in parallel: dual antigen targeting, armored CAR-T (secreting cytokines), stroma-degrading enzymes, and tumor-specific antigen discovery.

## Evidence Framework
- Hematologic: multiple Phase 3 RCTs, FDA approvals, durable remissions
- Solid tumors: mostly Phase 1, no durable responses at scale

VERDICT: PASS
