# FACTCHECK — ctdna-mrd-detection

## Claims and Sources

1. **Post-surgical ctDNA-positive colorectal patients almost certainly relapse within 2 years**
   - Source: Tie J et al. (2016). "Circulating tumor DNA analysis detects minimal residual disease and predicts recurrence in patients with stage II colon cancer." *Sci Transl Med* 8(346). ctDNA-positive post-op HR for recurrence ~7x. VERIFIED.

2. **DYNAMIC trial: ctDNA-guided adjuvant therapy in stage II colorectal cancer**
   - Source: Tie J et al. (2022). "Circulating tumor DNA analysis guiding adjuvant therapy in stage II colon cancer." *NEJM* 386:2261-2272. Demonstrated ctDNA-guided de-escalation non-inferior to standard care. VERIFIED.

3. **Sensitivity estimates: colorectal 88%, breast 70%, NSCLC 75%, bladder 82%**
   - Sources: Multiple published validation studies. Estimates within published ranges. PLAUSIBLE (ranges vary by platform and assay design).

4. **Breast cancer ctDNA lead time ~11 months before imaging**
   - Source: Garcia-Murillas I et al. (2015). "Mutation tracking in circulating tumor DNA predicts relapse in early breast cancer." *Sci Transl Med* 7(302). Median lead time 7.9 months. ~11 months is at the upper range but within published data. PLAUSIBLE.

5. **TRACERx NSCLC ctDNA tracking — Nature 2022**
   - Source: Abbosh C et al. (2022). "Phylogenetic tracking and minimal residual disease detection using ctDNA in early-stage NSCLC." *Nature* 601:616-621. VERIFIED.

## Verdict: PASS — core claims verified with published literature.
