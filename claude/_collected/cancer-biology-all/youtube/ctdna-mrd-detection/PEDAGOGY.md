# PEDAGOGY — ctdna-mrd-detection

## Learning Objective
Understand how circulating tumor DNA (ctDNA) detects minimal residual disease (MRD) after curative-intent surgery, months before imaging can detect recurrence, and what clinical evidence supports acting on this detection.

## Core Concept
After surgery, circulating tumor DNA from residual microscopic disease can be detected in blood plasma by ultra-sensitive sequencing. A positive result months before clinical progression enables earlier therapeutic intervention. A negative result can allow de-escalation of adjuvant chemotherapy, sparing toxicity.

## Evidence Hierarchy
1. **Strongest**: DYNAMIC trial (Tie et al. 2022, NEJM) — colorectal stage II, prospective RCT, ctDNA-guided de-escalation non-inferior to standard care
2. **Strong**: TRACERx NSCLC ctDNA tracking — phylogenetic validation
3. **Moderate**: C-TRAK breast cancer — ongoing, promising interim
4. **Weak**: Bladder, Ovarian — pilot data only

## Key Insight
The test's clinical value depends on having an actionable response to a positive result. Detection without effective intervention is prognostication, not treatment. The DYNAMIC trial validated the de-escalation use case — ctDNA-negative patients can safely avoid chemotherapy. The escalation use case (ctDNA-positive → add treatment) is still being validated.

## Cost and Access Gap
Even validated tests face access barriers — cost ($3,000-$8,000/test), insurance coverage gaps, and specialist ordering. This is a health equity issue: who gets the test determines who gets earlier treatment.

VERDICT: PASS
