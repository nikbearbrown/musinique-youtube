- B02: "The antibody works beautifully in the lab. The tumor data tells a different story. The targeting ligand made almost no difference to how much drug reached the tumor." — PASS.
- B03: "A ligand that binds cells ten times better in culture — yet doesn't increase how much drug reaches the tumor. The ligand is working. Why doesn't it help?" — PASS.
- B04: "A nanoparticle travels through a chain of environments to reach a cancer cell: blood, tumor vessel wall, tumor tissue — then, finally, the cell surface. Targeting acts only at the very last step." — PASS.
- B05: "Cell culture measures only step four — binding and entry — in conditions with no circulation, no vessel walls, no clearance. It cannot tell you what happens at steps one through three." — PASS.
- B06: "Total tumor accumulation is set by two earlier steps: how long the particle survives in circulation, and how permeable the tumor's blood vessels are. The ligand touches neither." — PASS.
- B07: "The ligand acts only after the particle has already arrived — at the cell surface, improving internalization. It cannot go back upstream and fix accumulation." — PASS.
- B08: "If accumulation is the bottleneck — too few particles reach the tumor — fixing cellular uptake does not help. You're improving the last step when the problem is upstream." — PASS.
- B09: "Two batches: folate-targeted and bare. Equal dose injected. Four hours later — tumor accumulation: 2.1% vs 1.9%. Nearly identical. But cellular internalization: 68% vs 12%. Same tumor, different cell entry." — PASS.
- B10: "Binds cells in a dish is not reaches a tumor in a body. The ligand acts last. Total accumulation is set upstream — by circulation and vessel permeability, not by binding." — PASS.
