# FACTCHECK — nanoparticle-characterization

| Claim | Source | Status |
|-------|--------|--------|
| DLS is standard size measurement; overestimates large particles (intensity-weighted) | Koppel DE, J Chem Phys 1972; DLS theory | VERIFIED |
| PDI < 0.2 widely cited as homogeneity target | ISO 22412:2017; formulation literature | VERIFIED |
| Zeta potential ±30 mV as colloidal stability indicator | Riddick TM, Control of Colloid Stability 1968; widely cited | VERIFIED |
| Encapsulation efficiency measured by dialysis/centrifugation | Multiple NP characterization methods papers | VERIFIED |

## Exclusions honored
- Full regulatory submission pathway for biologics: NOT covered
- Manufacturing GMP process details: NOT covered
- Clinical trial phases for nanomedicines: NOT covered

## Risk flags
- PDI < 0.2 is a guideline from the formulation literature, not a fixed FDA specification — state as widely cited target, not a regulatory hard cutoff.
- Zeta potential ±30 mV is a general rule of thumb; the actual threshold varies by particle type and media ionic strength.
