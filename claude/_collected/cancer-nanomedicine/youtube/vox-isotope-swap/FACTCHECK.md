## Terms table

| Term | Definition used in video | Chapter line reference |
|------|--------------------------|------------------------|
| PSMA | Prostate-specific membrane antigen — a transmembrane protein expressed on prostate cancer cells; the shared target for both imaging and therapy | Ch.7: "Prostate-specific membrane antigen is a transmembrane protein expressed at high levels on prostate cancer cells." |
| Theranostics | Combining therapy and diagnostics in a single molecular framework — same molecule (or matched sister) used for imaging and treatment | Ch.7: "Theranostics combines therapy and diagnostics in a single molecular framework." |
| Radioligand | A small targeting molecule carrying a radioisotope | Ch.7: "a small targeting molecule — the ligand — carries a radioisotope" |
| Gallium-68 (Ga-68) | PET isotope used on the PSMA targeting molecule for imaging | Ch.7: "Gallium-68-PSMA-11 … FDA-approved for prostate cancer staging and restaging" |
| Lutetium-177 (Lu-177) | Therapeutic beta-emitting isotope used on the identical PSMA targeting molecule for treatment | Ch.7: "The therapy side uses the same targeting molecule loaded with lutetium-177" |
| Isotope swap | Swapping the imaging isotope (Ga-68) for a therapeutic isotope (Lu-177) on the identical targeting molecule | Ch.7: "Swap the imaging isotope for a therapeutic isotope on the same ligand and you have a matched diagnostic/therapeutic pair." |
| Companion diagnostic | Imaging integrated into therapy as a mandatory patient-selection step tied mechanistically to the therapeutic target | Ch.7: "The imaging step becomes a genuine companion diagnostic for that specific therapy, not a generic staging procedure." |
| Patient selection | The imaging step screens patients whose tumors express the target, excluding those who would receive toxicity without benefit | Ch.7: "The PSMA scan is not a formality before treatment; it is the patient-selection step." |
| PSMA-negative | Tumor lesions that do not express PSMA; the drug cannot bind them; they do not respond | Ch.7: "several large, symptomatic lesions show almost no PSMA signal. They have dedifferentiated and lost the target." |

## THE EXAMPLE beat — illustrative numbers register

Beat B11 uses the following invented numbers, all labeled as illustrative:
- 6 total lesions (illustrative)
- 4 PSMA-positive (light on scan) (illustrative)
- 2 PSMA-negative (dark on scan) (illustrative)
- 60% shrinkage in PSMA-positive lesions at day 90 (illustrative)
- 40% growth in PSMA-negative lesions at day 90 (illustrative)
- 90-day restaging timepoint (illustrative)

These numbers are directionally consistent with the chapter's mechanism (PSMA-expressing lesions respond; PSMA-negative lesions do not) but are not drawn from any clinical trial.
