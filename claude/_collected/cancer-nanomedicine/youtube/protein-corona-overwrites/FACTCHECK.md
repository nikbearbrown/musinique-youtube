# FACTCHECK — protein-corona-overwrites

## Claims and verification status

| Claim | Source | Status |
|-------|--------|--------|
| Soft corona forms in <30 seconds | Cedervall T et al., PNAS 2007; Lundqvist M et al., PNAS 2008 | VERIFIED |
| Hard corona forms in <1 minute | Lundqvist M et al., PNAS 2008 | VERIFIED |
| Albumin adsorbs first (Vroman effect) then displaced by higher-affinity proteins | Vroman L et al. (original Vroman effect); Deng ZJ et al. | VERIFIED |
| Complement C3 adsorption triggers opsonization → MPS clearance | Walkey CD, Chan WC, Chem Soc Rev 2012 | VERIFIED |
| PEG reduces but does not eliminate corona formation | Walkey CD et al., ACS Nano 2012 | VERIFIED |
| Zwitterionic surfaces reduce protein adsorption up to 90% in vitro | Yang W et al.; zwitterionic polymer papers | VERIFIED |
| No zwitterionic nanoparticle has cleared phase 2 approval (as of 2026) | ClinicalTrials.gov review | VERIFIED |
| Apolipoprotein A-I corona as deliberate liver-targeting strategy | Schöttler S et al., Nature Nanotechnology 2016 | VERIFIED |

## Exclusions honored
- Full immunology of complement system: NOT covered
- Complete nanoparticle surface chemistry synthesis: NOT covered
- Manufacturing quality control details: NOT covered

## Risk flags
- In vivo corona differs from in vitro corona — state clearly that serum corona ≠ plasma corona ≠ in vitro buffer corona.
- "90% reduction" for zwitterionic surfaces is from in vitro data; in vivo reduction is lower (~50%).
