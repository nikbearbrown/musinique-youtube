# FACTCHECK — lnp-endosomal-escape

## Claims and verification status

| Claim | Source | Status |
|-------|--------|--------|
| Only 1-2% of mRNA LNP cargo escapes the endosome | Sahay G et al., Nature Biotechnology 2013; Gillmore et al. | VERIFIED |
| 98-99% of LNP cargo is degraded in lysosomes | Sahay 2013; endosomal release quantification literature | VERIFIED |
| LNP four components: ionizable lipid, phospholipid, cholesterol, PEG-lipid | Kulkarni JA et al., Nature Nanotechnology 2021 | VERIFIED |
| Ionizable lipid mechanism: neutral at pH 7.4, cationic at pH 5.5 → membrane disruption | Semple SC et al.; ionizable lipid mechanism papers | VERIFIED |
| COVID-19 mRNA vaccine LNPs use ionizable lipids: ALC-0315 (BNT162b2), SM-102 (mRNA-1273) | Pfizer/Moderna EMA assessment reports | VERIFIED |
| Onpattro (patisiran) is the first approved LNP drug (2018) | FDA approval August 2018 | VERIFIED |
| Optimal ionizable lipid pKa for systemic delivery: 6.2-6.5 | Jayaraman M et al., Angew Chem 2012; Semple et al. | VERIFIED |
| GALA fusogenic peptide improves endosomal escape | Wyman TB et al.; fusogenic peptide literature | VERIFIED |

## Exclusions honored
- CRISPR delivery details: NOT covered
- mRNA manufacturing process: NOT covered
- Immune stimulation by mRNA (innate immune activation): NOT covered
- siRNA vs. mRNA mechanism differences: NOT covered

## Risk flags
- 1-2% escape estimate varies by cell type and LNP formulation — state as approximate quantitative estimate.
- PEG-lipid shedding kinetics are formulation-dependent; ALC-0159 sheds faster than DSPE-PEG2000.
