# FACTCHECK — nanomedicine-translation-gap

## Claims and verification status

| Claim | Source | Status |
|-------|--------|--------|
| Fewer than 20 nanoparticle formulations have reached clinical approval after 30 years | Bobo D et al., Bioconjugate Chem 2016; update reviews | VERIFIED |
| Doxil (1995) primary benefit is reduced cardiotoxicity (LVEF preservation) | FDA label; Gabizon A et al.; cardiotoxicity comparison trials | VERIFIED |
| Doxil efficacy not superior to free doxorubicin for OS | Gordon AN et al., JCO 2001; Piccart M et al. meta-analysis | VERIFIED |
| Abraxane albumin-SPARC/gp60 pathway rationale distinct from passive EPR | Desai N, AAPS J 2008; nab-paclitaxel mechanism | VERIFIED |
| SPARC actually predicts Abraxane response is debated | Multiple papers including Locker GY et al. | VERIFIED — debate acknowledged |
| ADCs are the most clinically successful nano platform in terms of recent approvals | FDA approval records 2013-2024 | VERIFIED |
| Pluvicto (2022) as clearest example of loop model | Sartor O et al., NEJM 2021; FDA approval March 2022 | VERIFIED |
| Loop model requires: quantifiable imaging + confirmed biodistribution + target-confirmed kill | Theranostics field review; Fendler WP et al. | VERIFIED |

## Exclusions honored
- Full regulatory pathway for nanomedicine approval: NOT covered
- Manufacturing scale-up details: NOT covered
- Comprehensive list of all failed nanoparticle formulations: NOT covered

## Risk flags
- "Fewer than 20 approvals" count varies by how ADCs are classified (as nanoparticles vs. biologics) — state as approximate.
- SPARC-Abraxane correlation is debated; state clearly it is not an established predictive biomarker.
