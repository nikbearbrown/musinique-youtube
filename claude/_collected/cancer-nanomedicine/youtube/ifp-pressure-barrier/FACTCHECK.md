# FACTCHECK — ifp-pressure-barrier

## Claims and verification status

| Claim | Source | Status |
|-------|--------|--------|
| Solid tumor IFP 20-60 mmHg (pancreatic up to 80 mmHg) | Jain RK, Science 1994; Boucher Y, Jain RK, Cancer Res 1992 | VERIFIED |
| Normal tissue IFP 0-3 mmHg | Jain RK; interstitial fluid pressure literature | VERIFIED |
| Elevated IFP caused by leaky vessels + absent functional lymphatics | Jain RK, PNAS 1987; Nature Reviews Cancer 2001 | VERIFIED |
| IFP reverses convection outward → only diffusion drives inward transport at tumor center | Jain RK, Science 2005; transport physiology | VERIFIED |
| Rim-dominant drug distribution in tumors (high drug periphery, low center) | Multiple tumor histology and drug distribution papers | VERIFIED |
| Jain vascular normalization hypothesis: anti-VEGF temporarily reduces IFP | Jain RK, Nature Reviews Cancer 2001; Tong RT et al. 2004 | VERIFIED |
| Normalization window 2-6 days in mouse models | Tong RT et al., Cancer Res 2004; Winkler F et al. | VERIFIED |
| Losartan depletes collagen via TGF-beta suppression → IFP reduction | Diop-Frimpong B et al., PNAS 2011; Jain lab NCT trials | VERIFIED |

## Exclusions honored
- Full anti-angiogenic therapy clinical outcomes: NOT covered
- VEGF receptor signaling in detail: NOT covered
- Lymphangiogenesis in cancer: NOT covered

## Risk flags
- Normalization window timing (2-6 days) is from mouse models; human window is poorly defined and variable — state clearly.
- No survival benefit from losartan strategy demonstrated in published clinical trials yet — state "clinical data from NCT trials show improved delivery, but no OS benefit yet."
