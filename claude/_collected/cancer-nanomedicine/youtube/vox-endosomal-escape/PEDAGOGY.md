### 5. Vocabulary law

Terms debut only after the beat that creates the need for them:

- "siRNA" appears in B01 as the paradox object — viewer has just been told it works
  in a dish but not in a mouse; the term names the drug they need to track.
- "endosome" is named in B02 as the bubble that traps the siRNA — viewer has just
  learned the siRNA never reaches the cytosol; now they learn what trapped it.
- "endocytosis" debuts in B04 as the process — viewer has established endosome from B02;
  now they learn how it forms.
- "ionizable lipid" debuts in B06 — viewer has established (B04–B05) that the endosome
  is acidic; now they need to know what can respond to that acidity.
- "amine" debuts in B06 within the ionizable lipid explanation — named only after the
  lipid itself is introduced.
- "protonated / cationic" debuts in B06 — the state the amine enters at low pH, after
  the pH drop was established in B05.
- "endosomal escape" debuts in B07 — the membrane crack is shown first; then the term
  names what just happened.

No term debuts before its need is established. PASS.
