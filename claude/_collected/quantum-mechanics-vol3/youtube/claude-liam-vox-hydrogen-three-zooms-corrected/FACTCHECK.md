# Fact Check

- The `n=2` binding scale is `3.4 eV`; it is a characteristic level scale, not the small splitting itself.
- The `2P3/2−2P1/2` fine-structure interval is about `4.5×10^-5 eV` (roughly 11 GHz).
- Dirac-Coulomb theory leaves `2S1/2` and `2P1/2` degenerate; the measured Lamb shift is about 1058 MHz or `4.4×10^-6 eV`, with `2S1/2` higher.
- Gross-to-fine is about 75,000×, not one decade. Fine-to-Lamb is about 10×.
- The Lamb shift is a radiative-QED result dominated by self-energy with vacuum-polarization and other corrections; “vacuum jitter” alone is an incomplete explanation.
- Spectrometers observe transition frequencies; level strokes require selection rules and linewidth/resolution context.

VERDICT: PASS
