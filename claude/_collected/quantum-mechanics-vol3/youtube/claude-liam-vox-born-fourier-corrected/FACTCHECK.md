# Fact check

- In the first Born approximation, the scattering amplitude is proportional to the 3D Fourier transform of the potential V at wavevector transfer q.
- For elastic scattering, |q| = 2k sin(theta/2); physical momentum transfer is h-bar q.
- A fixed incident k covers only 0 through 2k, not the full Fourier domain.
- Differential cross-section ordinarily gives |f|², so phase information is not directly supplied.
- Inverse reconstruction needs additional data or assumptions and is affected by noise and multiple scattering.
- For a Yukawa potential, V-tilde is proportional to 1/(q² + mu²).
- First Born can fail for strong scattering, low energy, or resonant behavior.
