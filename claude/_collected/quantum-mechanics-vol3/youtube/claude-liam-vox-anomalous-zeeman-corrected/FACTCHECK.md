# Fact Check

- The Landé formula `ΔE=g_J μ_B B m_J` is a weak-field LS-coupling result requiring Zeeman energy small compared with the relevant fine-structure splitting.
- For hydrogen `2P3/2`, `g_J=4/3`; adjacent `m_J` rungs differ by `(4/3)μ_BB`. The source chapter incorrectly calls the adjacent spacing `(2/3)μ_BB`.
- For `2P1/2`, `g_J=2/3`; the two `m_J=±1/2` rungs are separated by `(2/3)μ_BB` in total.
- Different upper/lower g factors and allowed `Δm_J=0,±1` transitions generate nonuniform frequency shifts; line intensities additionally require matrix elements, populations, polarization, and geometry.
- Intermediate fields require joint diagonalization; strong fields approach the Paschen–Back basis `|m_l,m_s〉`.
