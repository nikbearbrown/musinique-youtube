# Factcheck

For an allowed electric-dipole transition in homogeneous free space,
`A_E1 = omega^3 |d|^2 / (3 pi epsilon_0 hbar c^3)`.
The photon density of states contributes `omega^2`; the squared one-photon electric-field amplitude contributes `omega`. The `2p -> 1s` hydrogen lifetime is approximately 1.6 ns. Comparing two hypothetical E1 transitions at 300 MHz and 600 THz with equal dipole magnitude gives a frequency ratio of two million and a rate ratio of eight times ten to the eighteenth.

A proton spin flip is magnetic-dipole, not E1. Experimental NMR relaxation is environment-dependent. Cavities and structured photonic environments alter the local density of states. These caveats are explicit.
