# Fact check

- Fermi's golden rule gives a weak-coupling transition rate into a smooth continuum: Gamma = 2 pi |V_fi|² rho(E_f) / h-bar.
- Linear total transition probability in first-order theory is an intermediate-time result; survival is initially quadratic.
- Exponential survival additionally uses a short-memory or Markov approximation and is not exact at all times.
- Finite or structured reservoirs can retain memory and produce recurrences or nonexponential decay.
- The hydrogen 2p lifetime is about 1.6 ns, corresponding to a rate near 6.3e8 per second.
- Doubling rho at fixed matrix element doubles Gamma and halves tau within the golden-rule regime.
