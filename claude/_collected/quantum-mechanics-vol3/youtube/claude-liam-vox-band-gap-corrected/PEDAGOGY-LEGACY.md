# PEDAGOGY — vox-band-gap

## Concept chain
1. Free electrons: E=ℏ²k²/2m parabola, all energies allowed. No gap.
2. Add lattice (period a): at k=π/a, forward and backward Bragg-reflected waves are equal amplitude. They're degenerate in energy.
3. Degeneracy → mix into two standing waves: ψ+ = cos(πx/a) and ψ- = sin(πx/a).
4. ψ+ peaks on ion positions; ψ- peaks between them. Same kinetic energy but different potential energy (ions are attractive centers).
5. Energy difference = 2|V_G|, where V_G is the first Fourier component of the lattice potential. This is the forbidden gap.
6. Fill bands: insulator (gap > E_F), semiconductor (small gap ~1 eV), metal (E_F cuts a band).

## Misconception barriers
- "The gap is kinetic energy splitting" — NO: same k, same kinetic energy; the split is purely potential.
- "Electrons scatter in a perfect crystal" — Bloch's theorem: periodic potential → NO scattering. Gap is not from scattering.
- "Semiconductor gap is like a box trap" — NO: electrons move freely within each band; only the gap energy region is forbidden.
