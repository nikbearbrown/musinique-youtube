# Fact Check

The source card's intuition is useful but its headline is too broad. A static periodic one-electron potential produces Bloch eigenstates and is not itself a random resistive scatterer. That statement does **not** imply that every perfect crystal conducts, that an ordinary ideal metal is a superconductor, or that electron-electron and Umklapp physics can always be ignored.

Corrections applied:

- Replaced “a perfect crystal has zero electrical resistance” with the narrower Bloch-state statement.
- Distinguished completely filled bands (elementary band insulators) from partially filled metallic bands.
- Described the no-relaxation metallic response as an ideal undamped/Drude-weight response, not as superconductivity.
- Named phase rigidity and the Meissner effect as extra superconducting physics.
- Added impurities, defects, phonons, boundaries, contacts, electron-electron scattering, and Umklapp as transport mechanisms or qualifications.
