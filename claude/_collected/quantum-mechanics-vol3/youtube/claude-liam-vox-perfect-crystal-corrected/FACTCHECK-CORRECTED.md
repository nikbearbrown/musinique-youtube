# Corrected Thesis

A static, perfectly periodic one-electron potential is part of the crystal Hamiltonian, so its stationary states are extended Bloch waves rather than free waves repeatedly and randomly scattered by individual ions. Finite resistance requires current-relaxing physics, but whether the ideal crystal conducts also depends on band filling and interactions. A filled band can be insulating; a partially filled noninteracting band can have an undamped ideal response; neither fact by itself makes the material a superconductor.
