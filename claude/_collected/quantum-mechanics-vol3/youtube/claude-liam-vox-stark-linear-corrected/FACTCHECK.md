# Fact Check

VERDICT: PASS

- The fourfold `n=2` degeneracy is explicitly identified as the ideal nonrelativistic Coulomb model; fine structure and the Lamb shift are omitted and later restored as a regime caveat.
- For a field along `z`, parity removes same-parity matrix elements and azimuthal symmetry imposes `Δm=0`.
- Two Hermitian-conjugate matrix entries remain, representing one independent real integral, `⟨2s|z|2p0⟩ = -3a0` up to state phase convention.
- The ideal first-order shifts are `±3a0 e E`, with the `m=±1` pair unshifted to first order.
- At `10^5 V/cm`, the shift magnitude is approximately 15.9 meV.
- Degeneracy of an energy value is not equated with twice the observed spectral intensity; transition and measurement conditions are required.
