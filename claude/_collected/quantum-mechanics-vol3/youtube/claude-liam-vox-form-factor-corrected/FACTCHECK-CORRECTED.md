# Fact Check

VERDICT: PASS

- A point Coulomb/Mott cross section is not isotropic; form-factor factorization multiplies that angular baseline by `|F(q)|²` under stated approximations.
- For a uniform sharp sphere, `F(q)=3j1(qR)/(qR)` and its first zero is `qR≈4.493`.
- The inferred `R` is a model sharp radius; for a uniform sphere only, `r_rms=sqrt(3/5)R`.
- Destructive interference is attributed to the integrated charge distribution, not a literal pairwise left-half/right-half cancellation.
- Experimental and theoretical corrections—diffuse surface, deformation, Coulomb distortion, relativity, inelastic background, resolution, and q coverage—are explicit.
- Proton radius is not claimed to come from a first dark ring; it is commonly extracted from the low-`Q²` form-factor slope.
- Exercise: `4.493/0.75=5.99 fm`; uniform-sphere RMS radius `4.64 fm`.
