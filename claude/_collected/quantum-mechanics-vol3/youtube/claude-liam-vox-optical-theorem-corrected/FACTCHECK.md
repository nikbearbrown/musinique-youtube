# Fact check

- An impenetrable sphere has low-energy scattering length a and elastic cross-section 4 pi a².
- At high energy, its total elastic cross-section approaches 2 pi a², with oscillatory corrections; this is the extinction paradox.
- The extra high-energy pi a² is associated with forward diffraction, distinct from the low-energy s-wave factor four.
- The optical theorem relates total cross-section to the imaginary part of the forward elastic amplitude; with reaction channels, total includes elastic plus reaction loss.
- This hard-sphere result is not a universal lower bound; Ramsauer-Townsend transparency is a counterexample, and unscreened Coulomb scattering needs special handling.
- For a = 2 Å: 4 pi a² ≈ 5.03e-19 m² and 2 pi a² ≈ 2.51e-19 m².
