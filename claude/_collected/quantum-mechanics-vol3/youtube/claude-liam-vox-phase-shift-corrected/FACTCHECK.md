# Fact Check

VERDICT: PASS

- Uses the standard asymptotic convention `u_l ~ sin(kr-lπ/2+δ_l)` and notes that `δ_l` is defined modulo `π`.
- In the weak-potential limit, the leading phase shift carries the opposite sign from a positive weighted integral of `V`; simple attraction therefore tends positive and repulsion negative.
- The wavelength story is restricted to propagating regions; a barrier above the incident energy is evanescent.
- Strong potentials, bound states, resonances, mixed-sign potentials, energy dependence, and phase wrapping are explicit limits on the sign shortcut.
- `sin²δ_l` reaches a partial-wave unitarity maximum at `π/2 mod π`; this is not by itself declared a resonance.
- One intensity pattern is not claimed to uniquely reconstruct a potential.
