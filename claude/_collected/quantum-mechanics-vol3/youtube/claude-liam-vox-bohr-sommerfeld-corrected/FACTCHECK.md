# Fact Check

VERDICT: PASS

- A classical harmonic-oscillator energy shell is an ellipse in `(x,p)` with enclosed action `J = ∮p dx = 2πE/ω`.
- The ellipse is explicitly a semiclassical energy-shell visualization, not a definite quantum particle trajectory or the exact ground-state distribution.
- For two ordinary soft turning points, the closed orbit has Maslov index `μ=2`, yielding `J=(n+μ/4)h=(n+1/2)h`.
- The harmonic oscillator is a special case where WKB reproduces the exact spectrum `E_n=(n+1/2)ℏω`.
- Convention-dependent local turning-point phase language is not used as an invariant claim.
- For `ω=10^14 rad/s`, `ℏω/2 = 0.0329 eV = 32.9 meV`.
