# Fact check

- With the convention P_Rabi = sin²(Omega t / 2), the first-order resonant result is P_1 = (Omega t / 2)².
- At a pi pulse, these give 1 and pi²/4 ≈ 2.467 respectively.
- The bounded sine-squared result is the resonant coherent two-level rotating-wave model, not an exact description of every driven atom.
- First-order validity is controlled by small transition probability and the requested error tolerance; 0.3 is not asserted as a universal cutoff.
- At Omega t = 1, the values are 0.25 and sin²(0.5) ≈ 0.2298, a relative overestimate of about 8.8% against the Rabi value.
