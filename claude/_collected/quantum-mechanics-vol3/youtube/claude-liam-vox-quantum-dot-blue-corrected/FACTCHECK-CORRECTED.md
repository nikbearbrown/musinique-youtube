# Corrected Thesis

Shrinking a semiconductor nanocrystal raises the leading kinetic-confinement contribution to its optical transition approximately as `1/R²`, so a controlled series often shifts toward higher-energy, shorter-wavelength absorption and emission. Quantitative colors require electron and hole masses, Coulomb attraction, finite-barrier and band-structure corrections, dielectric and surface physics, and a distinction between absorption and relaxed emission.
