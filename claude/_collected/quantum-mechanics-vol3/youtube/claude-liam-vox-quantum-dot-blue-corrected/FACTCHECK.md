# Fact Check

The card's qualitative trend is sound for a controlled semiconductor-quantum-dot series, but the particle-in-a-box story is only the leading effective-mass model.

Corrections applied:

- The optical excitation confines an electron and a hole, not one generic electron level.
- The positive confinement contribution scales as approximately `1/R²`; it is added to the bulk band gap.
- Electron–hole Coulomb attraction supplies a negative term scaling approximately as `1/R`.
- Bulk effective masses can fail for very small dots because the bands are nonparabolic at the sampled wave vectors.
- Finite barriers, dielectric environment, shape, size distribution, and surface chemistry affect the spectrum.
- Absorption and emission were separated; carrier relaxation, Stokes shifts, and trap emission prevent a universal one-to-one radius/color rule.

VERDICT: PASS
