# Fact check — H2+ chemical bond

- Scope: qualitative Born–Oppenheimer, one-electron H2+ LCAO model.
- The normalized symmetric combination has constructive overlap and enhanced internuclear density; the antisymmetric combination has a midplane node.
- Binding is determined by the full energy, including electronic kinetic energy, electron–nuclear attraction, and nuclear repulsion.
- The bonding H2+ curve has an equilibrium separation near 1.3 Å and dissociation energy near 1.8 eV; the antibonding curve has no binding minimum.
- The video deliberately avoids claiming that internuclear density alone is a complete universal explanation of every covalent bond.
