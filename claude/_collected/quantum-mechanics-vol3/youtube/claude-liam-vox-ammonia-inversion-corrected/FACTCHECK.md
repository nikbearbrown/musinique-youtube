# Fact Check

- The commonly cited ammonia inversion transition is near 23.87 GHz.
- `hν` at 23.87 GHz is approximately `9.87×10^-5 eV`; the source chapter's `9.94×10^-5 eV` is corrected.
- Symmetric and antisymmetric inversion states are stationary energy eigenstates. A localized left/right preparation is a superposition whose localization probability oscillates; describing an energy eigenstate as a nitrogen bead physically hopping 24 billion times per second is misleading.
- The first ammonia molecular-beam maser was demonstrated by Gordon, Zeiger, and Townes in 1954. State selection and a resonant cavity produced microwave amplification by stimulated emission.
- Exact frequencies depend on isotopologue and rotational/hyperfine state; the episode uses the familiar inversion line and does not claim one number for all ammonia transitions.

VERDICT: PASS
