# Corrected Thesis

Tunneling couples ammonia's two mirror-image pyramidal configurations, making symmetric and antisymmetric delocalized energy eigenstates separated by an inversion splitting. Their microwave transition lies near 23.87 GHz for the familiar line and powered the 1954 ammonia molecular-beam maser. A localized superposition can oscillate between left/right probability, but a stationary eigenstate is not a classical nitrogen bead hopping at that rate.
