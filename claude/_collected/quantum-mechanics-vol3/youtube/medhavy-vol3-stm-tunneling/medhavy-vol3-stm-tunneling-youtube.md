**The physics:** STM tunneling current follows I ∝ e^(−2κd), where κ = √(2mφ)/ℏ. For a typical metal work function φ = 4 eV, κ ≈ 1.024 Å⁻¹. The simulation verifies two results: the ln(I) vs d plot is a straight line with slope −2κ ≈ −2.05 Å⁻¹, and each additional angstrom of tip-to-sample gap multiplies the current by e^(−2κ·1Å) ≈ 1/7.7 — the Binnig-Rohrer rule that makes atomic-resolution imaging possible.
