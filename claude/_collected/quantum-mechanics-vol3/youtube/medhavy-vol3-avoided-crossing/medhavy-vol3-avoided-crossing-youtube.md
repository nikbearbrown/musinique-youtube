**The physics:** For a two-level system with E₁ = 0, E₂ = λ, and coupling V = 0.1 eV, the exact eigenvalues are E± = (E₁+E₂)/2 ± √((ΔE/2)² + V²). The simulation verifies two predictions: at the degeneracy point (λ = 1 eV) the minimum gap equals 2V = 0.2 eV exactly, and increasing V to 0.3 eV doubles the gap to 0.6 eV, confirming that the gap scales linearly with coupling strength (von Neumann–Wigner theorem).
