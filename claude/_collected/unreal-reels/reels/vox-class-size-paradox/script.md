# The Class-Size Paradox — Calling Bullshit (Vox Style), film three

Candidate 03 (weighted sampling / experienced mean). 12 beats, est. ~1:35.
Rotation: STILL 2 · CARD 4 · GRAPHIC 6, max run 2. Color law: navy = the
students, ink = the classrooms, golden highlighter = the brochure number
(the pen, once). Worked example verified: 20×20 + 4×200 = 1,200 students;
class-mean 50; student-experienced mean (8,000+160,000)/1,200 = 140.
Exclusions honored: no weighted-mean formula on screen, no US News subplot,
no friendship-paradox generalization.

**A01 · STILL·ai·kenburns** — packed lecture hall from the back row
> Your university says the average class is fifty students. It feels more
> like a hundred and forty. Here's the trick: both numbers are true.

**A02 · CARD** — CALLING BULLSHIT / THE CLASS-SIZE PARADOX / two true averages, one impression
> Two averages, honestly computed, wildly different. This is the story of
> how a university tells the truth and misleads you anyway.

**A03 · GRAPHIC A03_Campus** — grid of 24 classroom squares (20 small, 4 huge)
> Picture a tiny college: twenty-four classes. Twenty of them hold twenty
> students each. Four are monsters, with two hundred students apiece.

**A04 · GRAPHIC A04_ClassMean** — counter averages over classrooms → 50
> Average over the classes, and the administrator is right: twenty-four
> classrooms, mean size fifty. That's the number in the brochure.

**A05 · CARD** — THE OTHER QUESTION / AVERAGE THE STUDENTS, NOT THE CLASSES
> Now ask the other question: don't average the classrooms. Average the
> students' experience.

**A06 · GRAPHIC A06_StudentDrop** — a navy student-square drops in repeatedly
> Drop into this school as a random student, and odds are you land in a
> monster: two-thirds of all students are sitting in the four giant lectures.

**A07 · GRAPHIC A07_ExperiencedMean** — the counter recomputes → 140
> Average what students actually experience, and the number isn't fifty.
> It's one hundred and forty.

**A08 · CARD** — THE MECHANISM / SIZE-WEIGHTED SAMPLING / big classes hold more witnesses
> Big classes contain more students, so a random student lands in a big
> class more often than a random class is big.

**A09 · GRAPHIC A09_TwoAverages** — both counters side by side, same school
> Same school, same classes, no lies: the class average is fifty, the
> student average is one hundred forty. The choice of average is the message.

**A10 · STILL·ai·kenburns** — a glossy admissions brochure on a desk
> And guess which number makes the brochure.

**A11 · GRAPHIC A11_Everywhere** — isotype pair: full flights, crowded gyms
> The same trick makes your flights feel fuller and your gym feel busier —
> anywhere big groups hold more witnesses, experience outweighs the average.

**A12 · CARD** — THE RULE / ASK: AVERAGED OVER WHAT? / units decide the story
> So when someone quotes you an average, ask: averaged over what? The units
> chosen decide the story told.
