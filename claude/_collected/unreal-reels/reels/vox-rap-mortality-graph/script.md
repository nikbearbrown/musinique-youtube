**A01 · STILL·ai·kenburns** — cold open: boombox-and-microphone still life
> A few years ago, a chart went viral: jazz musicians live past sixty; rap
> musicians die at thirty. The data was real. The conclusion was garbage.

**A02 · CARD** — CALLING BULLSHIT / THE RAP MORTALITY GRAPH / a lie the data told itself
> This is the story of how honest numbers tell a lie — and of the one
> question that breaks the spell.

**A03 · GRAPHIC** — the viral chart, drawn (bars by genre)
> Here's the chart: average age at death, by genre. Jazz, blues, country,
> gospel: up past sixty. Rap and hip-hop: about thirty — half the others.

**A04 · GRAPHIC** — golden highlighter on the rap bar
> The gaps are huge, the pattern is clean, and the story writes itself:
> rap kills its stars.

**A05 · CARD** — the question card: WHO CAN BE IN A DEAD-MUSICIANS DATASET?
> But hold on. Ask the question the chart is hoping you won't: who can even
> be in a dataset of dead musicians?

**A06 · GRAPHIC** — genre ages timeline
> But the genres aren't the same age. Jazz, blues, country, gospel: a century
> old or more. Rap and hip-hop: forty years, at most.

**A07 · STILL·ai·kenburns** — the survivor: a man in his fifties at a mixing desk
> A rapper who started at twenty in the eighties is in his fifties right
> now — alive, and therefore invisible to this chart.

**A08 · GRAPHIC** — living musicians drop out of the dataset
> Living musicians can't appear in a dead-musicians dataset. So for a young
> genre, the only members who qualify are the ones who died young.

**A09 · GRAPHIC** — THE beat: the study-window line slams down; running lifespans vanish
> The study window slams shut at the present day, and every lifespan still
> running simply vanishes from the data.

**A10 · CARD** — THE MECHANISM / RIGHT-CENSORING / the window hides the living
> The trick has a name: right-censoring. The chart didn't measure how long
> rappers live — it measured how long rap has existed.

**A11 · GRAPHIC** — a century of jazz deaths vs four decades of rap survivors
> Jazz has had a century to accumulate deaths at every age. Rap has had four
> decades — populated mostly by survivors.

**A12 · GRAPHIC** — the null rebuild: identical lifespans + a chopped window
> Keep every lifespan identical and just chop the window: the doomed-looking
> gap rebuilds itself. Most of the viral pattern is exactly that.

**A13 · CARD** — TO BE FAIR / SHE FLAGGED IT / but no caveat survives a screenshot
> To be fair, the study's author acknowledged the problem in her article.
> But charts travel alone — no caveat survives the screenshot.

**A14 · CARD** — THE RULE / ASK HOW OLD THE CATEGORY IS / before you mourn it
> So when a chart says the young die young, first ask how old the category
> is — startups, new drugs, careers, all of it.
