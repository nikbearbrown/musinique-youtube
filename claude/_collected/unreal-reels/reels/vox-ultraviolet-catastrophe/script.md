## COLD OPEN

**B01 · STILL·archive·kenburns** — foundry, glowing iron
> Heat a bar of iron and it glows. Red, then orange, then yellow-white. Any
> iron, any ceramic, anything at that temperature — the same color, the same
> glow.

**B02 · CARD** — title: THE ULTRAVIOLET CATASTROPHE / why physics predicted infinite light
> By 1900, that glow had been measured to within one percent. The best theory
> in physics predicted it infinitely wrong. Literally infinitely.

## CONTEXT — Berlin, the measurement

**B03 · STILL·archive·kenburns** — Lummer & Pringsheim / PTR Berlin
> In Berlin, Otto Lummer and Ernst Pringsheim traced the spectrum of heated
> bodies with exquisite care. Smooth, hump-shaped curves, reproducible to a
> percent. The data was solid.

**B04 · GRAPHIC·drawon** — axes + measured hump (the chart arc begins)
> Every temperature drew the same shape: a hump. Hotter meant taller, with
> the peak sliding toward blue. Explaining this hump was the whole game.

## THE CLASSICAL ARGUMENT — clean, and doomed

**B05 · COMPOSITE·archive** — furnace/kiln interior plate + mode annotation
> The classical argument was clean. Take a glowing cavity. Count the ways
> light can vibrate inside it. That count is pure geometry — nobody disputed
> it.

**B06 · GRAPHIC·isotype** — equal shares
> Then classical physics pays every vibration the same wage: k T of energy,
> each. Equal shares. The same rule that ran the steam age flawlessly.

**B07 · GRAPHIC·drawon** — Rayleigh–Jeans fits, then runs away (crimson)
> Modes times wages gives the Rayleigh–Jeans law. At long wavelengths it fits
> the Berlin data. Then the mode count keeps climbing — and the prediction
> runs away.

**B08 · STILL·archive·kenburns** — hearth / hot poker
> Add every frequency and the total is infinite. Taken literally, a hot poker
> should flood your living room with ultraviolet. It plainly does not.

**B09 · GRAPHIC** — the diagnosis: which assumption breaks
> So which assumption breaks? Not the counting. Not equal shares — gases obey
> it beautifully. The killer is subtler: that energy flows continuously, in
> any amount whatsoever.

## THE TURN — Planck

**B10 · STILL·archive·kenburns** — Planck at his desk, ~1900
> Max Planck was not trying to start a revolution. He was trying to fit a
> curve — working backward from the data, and honest about it.

**B11 · GRAPHIC** — the chunk staircase
> The only fix that worked: the cavity's oscillators cannot hold just any
> energy. Only whole steps. Zero, one, two, three chunks — nothing in
> between.

## EQUATION TANGENT — E = hν (beat group; zones 2→3→4, no values claim)

**T01 · GRAPHIC·equation_tangent·sentences**
> One line carries the revolution: E equals h nu. A chunk's energy is the
> light's frequency times a new constant of nature. That equals sign is a
> ban.

**T02 · GRAPHIC·equation_tangent·glossary** (spotlight: h)
> E, the energy of one chunk. Nu, the frequency of the light. And h —
> Planck's constant — the exchange rate between them. Ten to the minus
> thirty-four. Tiny.

**T03 · GRAPHIC·equation_tangent·example** (re-entry in narration)
> At a forge's temperature, red chunks cost almost nothing. Ultraviolet
> chunks cost fifty times the thermal budget. The cavity cannot pay. High
> frequencies stay dark.

## RESOLUTION

**B12 · GRAPHIC·drawon** — Planck curve over the data; runaway greys out
> Planck's formula traced the measurements within one percent. Where
> classical physics worked, it agrees exactly. Where classical physics
> exploded, it dies away exponentially. No catastrophe.

**B13 · GRAPHIC** — the number beat: 10²⁰
> In the ultraviolet, the classical prediction is off by twenty orders of
> magnitude. Not wrong by a little. Wrong by a one with twenty zeros.

**B14 · DOCUMENT·archive** — Annalen der Physik 1901 scan, annotated
> He published in 1901, fitting his new constant to within one percent of
> today's value. The paper reads like an apology, not a manifesto.
