## What this reel teaches
The hard-steps model proposes that complex life requires several improbable transitions, with the probability of clearing all n steps scaling as time to the power n. With n approximately 6, most civilizations that ever arise will emerge near the end of their star's habitable window, making us extreme statistical outliers by arriving early. This early emergence, rather than a Great Filter, may explain the Fermi silence. The declining star-formation rate and the spreading-civilization deadline add further implications for why we see an empty galaxy today.
