# PEDAGOGY — medhavy-vox-packet-spreading

## What the learner already knows
Wave packet, superposition of momenta, the idea of different speeds.

## What this reel teaches
Why a free quantum wave packet inevitably spreads: a localized packet contains
a range of momenta, and because E ∝ k² the phase velocity is proportional to k,
so high-k components outrun low-k components and the packet fans apart. The
doubling time scales as mσ₀²/ℏ — heavier particles or wider initial packets
spread more slowly; tighter confinement means faster spreading. This is purely
quantum dispersion, not thermal disorder.

## Physics gate numbers
- E = ℏ²k²/2m → phase velocity v = ℏk/2m, proportional to k ✓ (ch.08)
- Tight σ₀ → wide Δk → large speed spread → fast fan-out ✓
- Width doubling time ∝ mσ₀²/ℏ ✓
- Photon: linear dispersion → no spreading; electron: quadratic → spreads ✓
- B09: electron σ₀=1nm → t_double≈0.3fs; σ₀=0.1nm → 3as (illustrative) ✓
