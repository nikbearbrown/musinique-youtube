## What this reel teaches
General relativity predicts that light is deflected by mass through angle δ = 4GM/c²b, twice the Newtonian prediction. When source, lens, and observer are perfectly aligned, light bending on all sides forms a complete Einstein ring with angular radius θ_E = √(4GM·D_LS/c²·D_L·D_S). Measuring θ_E from an image and knowing distances from redshifts directly yields the total lens mass — including dark matter — without any assumption about composition. Galaxy cluster lensing maps show mass-to-light ratios of 200–300, with only 10–20% in visible matter. The Bullet Cluster demonstrates dark matter is collisionless: the lensing-measured mass passed through the collision while the gas (X-ray emitting) lagged behind.
