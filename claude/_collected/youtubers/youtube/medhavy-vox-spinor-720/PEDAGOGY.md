## What the learner already knows
Rotation, qubit/spin state, interference.

## What this reel teaches
Why a spin-½ state requires a 720° rotation to return to itself: the qubit state
is parameterized by θ/2, so a 360° physical rotation advances the state angle by
only 180°, acquiring a factor of −1. Probabilities are unchanged (|−1|²=1), so
the sign is invisible to direct measurement — but it is detectable in interference.
Neutron interferometry (1975) measured the 720° period directly by rotating one
branch of a split beam and observing fringe shifts at 360° and reset at 720°.
