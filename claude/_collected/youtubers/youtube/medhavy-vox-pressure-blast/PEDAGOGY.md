## What this reel teaches
Pressure is energy per unit volume, so E ≈ P × V: a pressurized gas expanding into vacuum releases energy equal to its pressure times its volume. Earth's atmosphere at 10^5 Pa yields only 10^5 J per cubic meter — trivial. The solar core at 2.5×10^16 Pa yields 2.5×10^16 J per cubic meter, equivalent to roughly 6 megatons of TNT. The Sun's gravitational binding energy (what holds it together against this internal pressure) is approximately 3.8×10^41 J. Hydrostatic equilibrium — dP/dr = −GMρ/r² — means gravity perfectly counterbalances pressure gradient at every shell, keeping the bomb contained. Temperature plays almost no role in explaining the energy difference; pressure does.
