# PEDAGOGY — medhavy-vox-step-reflection

## What the learner already knows
Wave, wavelength, reflection, kinetic energy vs potential.

## What this reel teaches
Why a quantum particle partially reflects off a potential step even when it has
more than enough energy to pass: the wave function must match in value and slope
at the boundary; with two different wavelengths on either side the only solution
is to split — some amplitude reflects, some transmits. The cause is wavelength
mismatch (impedance mismatch), not energy deficit. A step down reflects just as
much as a step up of the same size; only the abruptness matters.

## Physics gate numbers
- Boundary conditions: ψ and dψ/dx continuous at step ✓ (ch.06)
- R = ((k₁−k₂)/(k₁+k₂))² — depends on k ratio, not energy sign ✓
- Gradual potential: R → 0; sharp step: R maximized ✓
- Step down reflects same as step up of equal magnitude ✓
- B09: E=4eV, step down 3eV → R ≈ 2% (illustrative) ✓
