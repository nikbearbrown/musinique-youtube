## What this reel teaches
The proton-proton chain has an energy generation rate scaling as T^4, while the CNO cycle scales as T^20. A 10% temperature increase multiplies the PP rate by 1.46 but the CNO rate by 6.4. Above approximately 1.3 solar masses, the CNO cycle dominates, concentrating energy generation so intensely in the core that the temperature gradient required for radiative transport exceeds the adiabatic gradient. The core becomes convectively unstable. This convective mixing turns fresh hydrogen into the burning zone, and the outer envelope remains radiative — an inversion of the Sun's structure.
