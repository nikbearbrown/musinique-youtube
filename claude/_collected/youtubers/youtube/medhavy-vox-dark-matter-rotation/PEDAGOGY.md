## What the learner already knows
Orbital mechanics, v = √(GM/r), concept of luminous mass

## What this reel teaches
For a planet orbiting a central mass, orbital speed falls as v = √(GM/r). If a galaxy's mass were concentrated in its luminous disk, outer stars should show Keplerian falloff. Vera Rubin and Kent Ford measured Andromeda's rotation curve in the 1970s and found flat velocities of 200–250 km/s at all radii. For speed to remain constant while radius increases, enclosed mass must grow proportionally to r — but luminosity stops growing at the disk edge. This demands a spherical dark matter halo extending well beyond the visible galaxy. For the Milky Way, halo mass is estimated at 5–10 times the visible mass. The NFW profile fits thousands of observed galaxies.
