## What this reel teaches
Why the wave function must be complex: direction of motion lives entirely in the
imaginary part (the winding of the helix). |ψ|² discards the winding direction —
it only uses the length — so two packets with identical probability clouds can
still be physically distinct (one moves right, one left). Real wave functions
cannot represent a single direction of travel; any real packet is secretly two
counter-traveling waves. Complexity is not mathematical decoration — it is how
momentum is encoded in the wave function.

## Physics gate numbers
- ψ = Ae^{ikx}: phase winds at rate k; k > 0 → rightward, k < 0 → leftward ✓ (ch.03)
- |ψ|² = |A|² — independent of k's sign; direction is invisible in the observable ✓
- Real packet = superposition of ±k components — cannot be a pure direction ✓
- iℏ ∂ψ/∂t: the i distinguishes future from past and right from left ✓
- B09: k₀ = ±5 nm⁻¹ Gaussian — same |ψ|², opposite helix (illustrative) ✓
