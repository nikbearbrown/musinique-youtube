## What this reel teaches
Momentum in quantum mechanics is the derivative operator p̂=−iℏ∂/∂x. Applying x̂ and p̂ in different orders yields a nonzero result [x̂,p̂]=iℏ, because the product rule of differentiation generates an extra term. This commutator is the algebraic root of the Heisenberg uncertainty relation σ_x·σ_p≥ℏ/2 — no measurement disturbance needed, just operator algebra.
