## What this reel teaches
The no-hair theorem states that a stationary black hole in general relativity is completely characterized by only three quantities: mass, angular momentum (spin), and electric charge. All other information about infalling matter — composition, temperature, shape, baryon number — radiates away as gravitational waves during collapse and leaves no trace. Two black holes with identical M, J, Q are physically identical regardless of what fell in. In practice, astrophysical black holes are electrically neutral, so only mass and spin (the Kerr metric) are needed. This connects to the information paradox: quantum unitarity appears violated if information is truly erased.
