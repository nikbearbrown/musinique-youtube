## What the learner already knows
Wave packet, Fourier components, group velocity, packet spreading (C12).

## What this reel teaches
When a free quantum packet spreads, its Fourier components sort in space by speed: high-k (fast) components move to the front, low-k (slow) to the rear. This creates a spatial gradient of local wavenumber — the front has shorter wavelength, the rear has longer. This is chirp. An electron packet with k₀=5 nm⁻¹, Δk=1 nm⁻¹ develops a 16% momentum gradient after 100 fs.

## Physics gate numbers
- Group velocity v = ℏk/m → high-k faster ✓ (ch. 08)
- k_local(x,t) ≈ k₀ + (x−v_g·t)/(ℏt/m) ✓
- After 100 fs: k_front ≈ 5.8 nm⁻¹, k_rear ≈ 4.2 nm⁻¹ (16% spread) ✓
