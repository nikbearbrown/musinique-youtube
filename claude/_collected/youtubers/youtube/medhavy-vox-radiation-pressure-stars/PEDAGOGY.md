## What this reel teaches
Two pressures support a star against gravity: gas pressure (P_gas ∝ T) and radiation pressure (P_rad ∝ T^4). Because T^4 grows faster than T, there is always a crossover; in cool stars gas dominates, but above approximately 40 solar masses radiation pressure becomes comparable. The Eddington luminosity L_Edd = 4πGMc/κ is the critical luminosity where radiation pressure exactly balances gravity; above it, the outer layers are blown off. Stars above ~150 solar masses cannot accumulate mass faster than they lose it, creating a natural mass ceiling. Luminous Blue Variable outbursts like Eta Carinae's 1840s eruption represent brief Eddington-limit exceedances. Population III stars (no metals, lower opacity) had a higher Eddington ceiling and may have reached thousands of solar masses.
