# PEDAGOGY — medhavy-vox-superposition-slosh

## What the learner already knows
Energy eigenstate, superposition, phase, probability density.

## What this reel teaches
Why a superposition of two stationary states produces time-dependent motion:
each eigenstate rotates in the complex plane at its own rate (E/ℏ); the angle
between the two clocks changes at the Bohr frequency (E₂−E₁)/ℏ; the cross-term
in |ψ|² oscillates at that frequency, making the probability density slosh back
and forth. The energy is unchanged; only position probability oscillates.

## Physics gate numbers

- Sloshing frequency: (E₂−E₁)/ℏ (Bohr frequency) ✓
- Cross-term |ψ₁ψ₂*| produces the oscillation; |ψ|² for each alone is frozen ✓
- Energy of the superposition is not definite (mix of E₁ and E₂) ✓
- B09: H atom n=1+n=2 beat ≈ 2.5×10¹⁵ Hz (illustrative) ✓
