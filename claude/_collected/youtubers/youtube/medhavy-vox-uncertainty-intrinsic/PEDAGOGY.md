## What this reel teaches
Why the uncertainty principle is intrinsic to the state, not a measurement
disturbance: measuring position and momentum on separate, untouched copies of
the same state still satisfies σ_x · σ_p ≥ ℏ/2. The source is the canonical
commutation relation [x̂, p̂] = iℏ — non-commuting operators cannot share a
common eigenbasis. The Gaussian saturates the bound exactly. The Robertson
inequality generalizes: σ_A · σ_B ≥ ½|⟨[A,B]⟩| for any observable pair.
