## What this reel teaches
White dwarfs are stellar remnants with no energy source, cooling predictably at roughly 0.01 K/yr early in their history. The coolest white dwarfs in the Milky Way today are around 4000–5000 K, making them approximately 10–12 billion years old. This cooling clock sets an independent lower bound on the age of the galactic disk. Crystallization — when carbon and oxygen ions lock into a solid lattice — releases latent heat and temporarily slows the cooling, a phenomenon confirmed by Gaia's detection of a pile-up in the white dwarf color-luminosity sequence.
