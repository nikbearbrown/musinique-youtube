## What this reel teaches
Zero-point energy (ℏ²/2mΔx²) from the uncertainty principle keeps helium atoms jiggling even at 0 K. For helium, this is ~10 meV — ten times the ~1 meV van der Waals binding energy. So helium atoms escape their lattice sites. Every other element: binding wins. Helium: quantum wins. Solid only above ~25 atm.

## Physics gate numbers
- ZPE ≈ ℏ²/(2m·(3Å)²) ≈ 10 meV for He in 3Å confinement ✓ (ch. 07)
- van der Waals binding per He atom ≈ 1 meV ✓
- Solid He requires ~25 atm pressure ✓
