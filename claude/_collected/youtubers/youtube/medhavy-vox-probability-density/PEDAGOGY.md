## What the learner already knows
The concept of probability between 0 and 1, density as quantity per unit volume/area/length (e.g. g/cm³), integration as area under a curve.

## What this reel teaches
|ψ(x)|² is a probability density — probability per unit length — not a probability. You must integrate over a measurement interval to get a dimensionless probability. A peak density of 2 nm⁻¹ is perfectly valid; integrating over ±0.1 nm gives ~0.33. Population density analogy: 27,000/km² is not a probability. The confusion costs every quantum mechanics calculation.
