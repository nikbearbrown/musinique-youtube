## What this reel teaches
Why a single electron — one at a time, no partner — produces an interference
pattern: the single electron's wave function passes through both slits
simultaneously, interferes with itself, and creates a probability distribution
whose fringes govern where each dot lands. λ = h/p determines fringe spacing.
