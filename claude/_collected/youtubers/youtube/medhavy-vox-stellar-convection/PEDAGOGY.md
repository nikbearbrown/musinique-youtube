## What this reel teaches
The Schwarzschild criterion states that convection occurs when the actual temperature gradient exceeds the adiabatic gradient. In the Sun, the PP chain (ε ∝ T^4) generates energy gently enough that radiation carries it without exceeding the adiabatic limit — the core stays radiative. The Sun's outer layers have high opacity (neutral hydrogen), forcing a steep radiative gradient that triggers convection, giving a convective envelope extending ~200,000 km deep. In stars above ~1.3 solar masses, the CNO cycle (ε ∝ T^20) concentrates energy so intensely in the core that the gradient exceeds the adiabatic threshold — the core convects. The outer envelope has stripped plasma with low opacity, so radiation carries it easily — the envelope is radiative. The two structures are exactly inverted.
