## What this reel teaches
The central pressure of a self-gravitating uniform sphere is P_center = (2π/3)Gρ²R², which grows as the square of the radius. Rocky material has a yield stress (compressive strength) of roughly 10^7–10^8 Pa. Setting central pressure equal to yield stress gives a threshold radius of approximately 200–400 km for rock, below which material strength holds the body in any shape, and above which gravity forces material to flow into a sphere. Phobos at 11 km is far below threshold and stays lumpy. Ceres at 470 km is above threshold and has achieved hydrostatic equilibrium. The IAU definition of dwarf planet maps directly to this physical threshold. Ice has a lower yield stress, so icy bodies achieve roundness at smaller radii (~100–200 km).
