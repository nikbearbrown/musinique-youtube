## What the learner already knows
Wave, wavelength, reflection and transmission, interference.

## What this reel teaches
Why a quantum barrier becomes perfectly transparent at specific energies: the wave
inside the barrier bounces off both walls; when the barrier width equals an integer
number of half-wavelengths inside, the two reflected waves return exactly out of
phase and cancel by destructive interference, driving T to 1. The same mechanism
underlies anti-reflection coatings on camera lenses.

## Physics gate numbers
- Two internal reflections interfere; cancellation when 2k₂L = nπ ✓ (ch.06)
- T = 1 exactly at resonance; reflection drops to zero ✓
- Anti-reflection coating analogy: identical physics, optical wavelengths ✓
- Resonance is sharp: small detuning from the condition → reflection jumps back ✓
- B09: L=0.3nm, V=5eV, E≈3eV → n=1 first resonance (illustrative) ✓
