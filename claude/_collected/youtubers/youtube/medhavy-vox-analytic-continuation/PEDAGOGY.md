## What this reel teaches
ζ(s) = Σ 1/n^s converges only for Re(s) > 1. Analytic functions have at most one extension to a larger connected domain (like a jigsaw puzzle with forced piece positions). The functional equation gives ζ(−1) = −1/12 via ζ(2). The notation "1+2+3+... = −1/12" means the continuation's value at s=−1, not an elementary sum.

## Key facts
- ζ(s) converges for Re(s) > 1; diverges for Re(s) ≤ 1 ✓
- Analytic functions: angle-preserving; at most one extension to larger domain ✓
- Jigsaw analogy: right-half pieces force left-half into unique positions ✓
- Functional equation: ζ(−1) computed from ζ(2) = π²/6; result = −1/12 ✓
- Casimir effect: physical measurement consistent with zeta regularization ✓
