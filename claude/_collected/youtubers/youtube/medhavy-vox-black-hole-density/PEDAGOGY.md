# PEDAGOGY — medhavy-vox-black-hole-density

## What the learner already knows
Schwarzschild radius formula, basic density = mass/volume

## What this reel teaches
The Schwarzschild radius scales linearly with mass (r_s = 2GM/c²), so volume scales as M³, and average density within the event horizon scales as 1/M². This means supermassive black holes like TON 618 (6.6×10^10 solar masses, r_s ≈ 1300 AU) have an average density of roughly 10^-9 kg/m³ — far less than air at 1.2 kg/m³. Crossing the event horizon of a supermassive black hole is locally unremarkable because the tidal forces there are gentle. The density-mass relationship also explains why supermassive black holes face a lower density barrier for formation by merging.
