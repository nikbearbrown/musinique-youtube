# PEDAGOGY — medhavy-vox-spinning-phase

## What the learner already knows

## What this reel teaches
Why an energy eigenstate is called "stationary" even though its wave function is
constantly rotating in the complex plane: the time factor e^{−iEt/ℏ} spins the
phase at rate E/ℏ, but probability density |ψ|² depends only on the length of the
complex amplitude — which is time-independent. The wave function is a spinning
clock; |ψ|² is its shadow on the floor; the shadow holds still.

## Physics gate numbers
- Time factor: e^{−iEt/ℏ} rotates phase at rate E/ℏ ✓ (ch.04)
- |ψ|² = |ψ|² — squaring removes the phase, leaving a constant ✓
- Stationary means: all expectation values time-independent ✓
- Mix two eigenstates → different rotation rates → shadow shifts (no longer stationary) ✓
- B09: 1 eV ground state → phase rate ≈ 1.5×10¹⁵ Hz (illustrative) ✓
