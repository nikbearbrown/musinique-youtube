## What the learner already knows

## What this reel teaches
The Compton effect (1923): X-rays scattered from electrons come back with a longer wavelength. The shift Δλ=(h/mₑc)(1−cosθ) matches the photon-as-particle collision model exactly (2.4 pm at 90°). Classical wave theory predicts zero shift. Photons carry momentum p=h/λ; elastic collision with an electron transfers energy and momentum, stretching the wavelength.

## Physics gate numbers
- Δλ = (h/mₑc)(1−cosθ) ✓ (ch. 01)
- At θ=90°: Δλ = h/mₑc = 2.426 pm, measured 2.4 pm ✓
- At θ=180°: Δλ = 4.85 pm (maximum) ✓
- p = h/λ for photon ✓
