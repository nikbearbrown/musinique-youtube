# FACTCHECK — Who was Albert Einstein?

All claims verified against standard physics history sources.

## Verified claims

| Claim | Verdict | Note |
|---|---|---|
| Born Ulm 1879 | ✓ | Born March 14, 1879, Ulm, Kingdom of Württemberg |
| Patent clerk in Bern | ✓ | Swiss Federal Institute of Intellectual Property, 1902–1909 |
| Five papers in 1905 | ✓ | Four major papers + doctoral thesis (July 1905, U. Zurich) |
| Photoelectric effect paper March 1905 | ✓ | Submitted March 17, received by Annalen der Physik |
| Brownian motion paper May 1905 | ✓ | Submitted May 11, 1905 |
| Special relativity paper June 1905 | ✓ | Submitted June 30, 1905 |
| E=mc² paper September 1905 | ✓ | Submitted September 27, 1905 ("Does the Inertia of a Body Depend Upon Its Energy-Content?") |
| Nobel Prize 1921 for photoelectric effect | ✓ | Nobel in Physics 1921 (awarded 1922); citation specifically names photoelectric effect, NOT relativity |
| "God does not play dice" — 1926 | ✓ | From letter to Max Born, December 4, 1926: "Der Alte würfelt nicht" |
| Left Germany 1933 | ✓ | Was in US (Caltech) when Hitler became chancellor (Jan 1933); resigned German citizenship |
| Princeton Institute for Advanced Study | ✓ | Joined IAS 1933, remained until death |
| American citizen 1940 | ✓ | Naturalized October 1, 1940 |
| Died 1955 | ✓ | Died April 18, 1955, Princeton, NJ |
| Asked to be cremated, no monument | ✓ | Per his wishes; ashes scattered at undisclosed location |
| Brain removed without consent | ✓ | Pathologist Thomas Harvey removed brain during autopsy without family consent |
| c² ≈ 9×10^16 m²/s² | ✓ | c = 2.998×10^8 m/s, c² = 8.988×10^16 ≈ 9×10^16 m²/s² |
| General relativity 1915 | ✓ | Field equations published November 25, 1915 |
| EPR paradox 1935 | ✓ | Einstein, Podolsky, Rosen paper, Physical Review, May 1935 |
| Einstein "at war with quantum mechanics" | ✓ | Historical consensus; debated Bohr extensively 1927–1935 |

## Corrections applied to narration

- "God does not play dice" correctly attributed to 1926, not 1935 (which is the EPR paper date)
- Nobel 1921 "awarded" technically in 1922 (postponed from 1921); narration says "won ... 1921" which refers to the prize year, not ceremony — acceptable convention
- "No university position" in 1905: correct; his 1902–1909 post was civil servant, not academic
