# FACTCHECK — Who was Max Planck?

## Claimed facts (each beat)

**B00 — Cold open**
- Max Planck was conservative and meticulous ✓ — biographically attested; he called himself a reluctant revolutionary
- The central question: how does a hot object glow ✓ — blackbody radiation problem, accurate framing

**B01 — The Ultraviolet Catastrophe**
- Late 1800s physicists: correct timeframe — Rayleigh-Jeans law published 1900, crisis building from 1890s ✓
- Classical prediction: spectrum blows up at short wavelengths ✓ — the Rayleigh-Jeans law diverges as λ→0
- Term "ultraviolet catastrophe" ✓ — coined by Ehrenfest, applied retroactively to the pre-1900 problem
- "Infinite energy from a warm oven" — hyperbolic but physically correct description of the Rayleigh-Jeans divergence ✓

**B02 — The Gambit**
- "1900" ✓ — Planck presented his quantum hypothesis to the Berlin Physical Society, 14 December 1900
- "Act of desperation" ✓ — Planck's own words in a 1931 letter to Robert Wood
- E = hν ✓ — Planck's energy quantization formula; correct
- h (Planck's constant) ✓ — calibrated from experimental blackbody data
- "One of the fundamental constants of the universe" ✓ — h is indeed a NIST fundamental constant

**B03/B04 — ASK→RESULT (Planck curve)**
- Manim plot shows 5000K blackbody ✓ — representative temperature (surface of a K-type star, roughly)
- Planck distribution peaks ~580nm for 5000K ✓ — Wien's law: λ_max = 2898μm·K / 5000K ≈ 580nm
- Rayleigh-Jeans diverges at short wavelengths ✓ — mathematical fact
- "Matches every measurement ever taken" — strong claim but accurate for blackbody radiation across all wavelengths ✓

**B05 — The Reluctant Revolutionary**
- Planck spent years trying to undo the quantum ✓ — attested in multiple biographies; Planck was philosophically committed to classical continuity
- Einstein 1905 ✓ — photoelectric effect paper, Annalen der Physik, March 1905
- Light in quanta (photon) ✓ — Einstein's contribution; Planck himself had only quantized matter oscillators, not the field
- "Planck cracked open a door he didn't want to open" — accurate metaphor, attested in historiography ✓

**B06 — The Legacy**
- 1905 Einstein photoelectric effect ✓
- 1913 Bohr atomic model ✓
- 1925–27 Heisenberg/Schrödinger/Dirac quantum mechanics ✓
- 1947 Bell Labs transistor ✓ (Bardeen, Brattain, Shockley — uses quantum mechanics of semiconductors)
- 1960 Maiman first laser ✓ (Theodore Maiman, Hughes Research Laboratories)
- 1973 Lauterbur MRI ✓ (Paul Lauterbur, Nature paper; MRI uses nuclear spin quantum properties)

**B07 — The Man**

- Lost a son in WWI ✓ — Karl Planck, killed in action at Verdun, 1916

- Stayed in Germany ✓ — unlike many contemporaries who emigrated; Planck remained
- Argued for Jewish colleagues ✓ — met Hitler in 1933 to argue for Jewish scientists; Hitler dismissed him
- Bombed out ✓ — Planck's home in Grunewald destroyed 1944
- "Rebuilt the German physics community at 88" — Planck helped found the Max Planck Society in 1948 at age 89-90; he died in October 1947, so he DID NOT actually live to found it. **CORRECTION NEEDED**: The Max Planck Society was named after him posthumously. At 88 Planck was active in rebuilding/planning. Soften the claim.
- Died 1947 ✓ — October 4, 1947, Göttingen
