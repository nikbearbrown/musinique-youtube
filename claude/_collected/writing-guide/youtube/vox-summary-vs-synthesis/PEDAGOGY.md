## Key-case cold open

B01: "You read ten sources carefully. You cite all of them correctly. And you have not made a single argument." — concrete situation: a student has done the work (ten sources, correct citations) and still failed the core task. The failure is specific and relatable. No thesis, no verdict.

B02: "Most first drafts of argumentative research papers are not arguments." — stakes. Still no thesis given.

PASS — the cold open shows the failure mode without naming the answer. No thesis before B03.

## Gap formula on THE QUESTION beat

B03: "What is the difference between summarizing sources and synthesizing them — and why does the second require a move no single source can make for you?"

The question is named on screen (card copy) AND in narration. The gap: you can do the right things (read sources, cite correctly) and still not have an argument. The question frames the mystery.

PASS.
