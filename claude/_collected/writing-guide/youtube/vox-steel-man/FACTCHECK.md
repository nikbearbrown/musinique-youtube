## Terminology verification

**"straw man"**: standard rhetorical term for a weakened misrepresentation of an opponent's argument, constructed to be easily refuted. Chapter 12 uses it directly ("straw-manning"). Origin as a rhetorical term is well established in informal logic. ✓

**"steel man"**: the practice of engaging with the strongest possible version of the opposing argument, rather than the weakest. Chapter 12 uses it directly ("steel-manning"). The term appears in academic rhetoric and philosophy discourse. ✓

**"position argument"**: the genre name used in the chapter. Equivalent to "argumentative essay," "argumentative writing," or "position paper" in other writing pedagogy traditions. Chapter 12 uses this term throughout. ✓

**"counterargument"**: standard writing-pedagogy term for opposing arguments engaged in the objection section. Used throughout Chapter 12. ✓
