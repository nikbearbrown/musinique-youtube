# Fact Check

- For a normalized trial state in the Hamiltonian's domain, `⟨H⟩≥E₀` for the same Hamiltonian.
- Expanding `|ψ⟩=Σcₙ|n⟩` gives `⟨H⟩=Σ|cₙ|²Eₙ`; therefore `⟨H⟩−E₀=Σ|cₙ|²(Eₙ−E₀)≥0`.
- Equality requires support only in the ground-energy eigenspace; this wording covers possible degeneracy.
- For the simple helium product trial with effective charge `Z*`, `E(Z*)=Z*²−(27/8)Z*` Hartree.
- Its minimum is at `Z*=27/16=1.6875`, with `E=−729/256=−2.84765625 Ha≈−77.49 eV` using `1 Ha≈27.2114 eV`.
- At `Z*=2`, `E=−2.75 Ha≈−74.83 eV`.
- The exact nonrelativistic infinite-nuclear-mass helium ground energy is approximately `−2.903724 Ha≈−79.01 eV`; both trials are above it.
- Experimental energies include finite-mass, relativistic, and QED effects, so the narration compares against the exact energy of the same nonrelativistic Hamiltonian.
