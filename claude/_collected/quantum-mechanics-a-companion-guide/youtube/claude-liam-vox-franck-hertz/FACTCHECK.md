# Fact Check

- The Franck–Hertz experiment with mercury shows successive collector-current structures separated by about `4.9 V`, corresponding to an excitation energy near `4.9 eV`.
- The robust observable is the spacing; contact potentials and apparatus conditions can shift absolute feature positions.
- Below the inelastic threshold, collisions are predominantly elastic and electron energy loss to atomic center-of-mass recoil is small because mercury is much heavier than an electron.
- Above threshold, electron-impact excitation can transfer the discrete excitation energy to the atom.
- Current minima arise when many electrons undergo an inelastic collision late enough that they cannot overcome the retarding potential; this is an ensemble statement, not an identical trajectory for every electron.
- Further voltage increments permit recovery and then additional excitations, generating approximately periodic structure.
- The Your Turn answer is `7.0−4.9=2.1 eV`, conditional on one such excitation collision.
- Mercury fine structure, line assignments, detailed tube geometry, and emission spectroscopy are outside scope.
