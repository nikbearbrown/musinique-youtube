# Fact check

VERDICT: PASS

- For a two-level Hamiltonian with diabatic energies crossing linearly and real coupling g, the adiabatic energies separate by 2√((Δ/2)²+g²); the minimum gap at Δ=0 is 2|g|.
- Nonzero coupling produces an avoided crossing and exchange of diabatic state character across the mixing region.
- At g=0 the curves cross exactly. Crossings can also be protected when symmetry forbids coupling between sectors.
- The perturbative mixing coefficient and second-order energy shift grow as unperturbed gaps shrink, but non-degenerate perturbation theory itself breaks down at degeneracy; the video uses this only as intuition away from the center.
- Excited-state second-order shifts need not have a universal sign, so the narration does not claim all individual levels always move in one direction.
