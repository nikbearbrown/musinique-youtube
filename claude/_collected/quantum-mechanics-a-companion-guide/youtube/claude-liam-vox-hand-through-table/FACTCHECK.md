# Fact check

VERDICT: PASS

- Electron clouds are spatial quantum states, not literal hollow shells.
- Ordinary contact resistance involves electromagnetic interactions and the fermionic antisymmetry constraint.
- Pauli exclusion is not an additional force term; “exchange correlation” is preferred to “exchange force.”
- Two electrons may share a spatial orbital only when their full spin-orbitals differ.
- Whole-hand tunneling is not mathematically forbidden but is negligible on ordinary timescales.

Source: `chapters/08-identical-particles.md`, especially exchange symmetry, Pauli exclusion, and exchange correlation.
