# Fact check

VERDICT: PASS

- For a Laplacian eigenmode, diffusion produces a real decaying factor exp(−Dk²t); free Schrödinger evolution produces a pure phase exp(−iℏk²t/2m) of unit modulus.
- Diffusion preferentially damps high-k structure and is not stably time-reversible.
- Schrödinger evolution under a self-adjoint Hamiltonian is unitary, norm-preserving, and reversible in the ideal closed-system model.
- A free Gaussian quantum packet generally spreads; the video explicitly corrects the legacy script’s “stays sharp” claim.
- Total probability preservation does not mean probability inside every fixed spatial region stays constant.
