# Fact Check

- Bohr's model and the nonrelativistic Schrödinger Coulomb Hamiltonian both give `E_n=−13.6 eV/n²` for hydrogen to the usual rounded precision.
- Bohr assumes circular trajectories and `L=nℏ`; modern quantum mechanics uses stationary wave functions and has no electron trajectory in an energy eigenstate.
- In the pure Coulomb problem, energy depends only on principal `n`; the enlarged degeneracy is associated with the conserved Runge–Lenz structure and dynamical `SO(4)` symmetry for bound states.
- For `n=2` ignoring spin, there is one `2s` spatial state and three `2p` magnetic spatial states, all at `−3.4 eV` in the ideal nonrelativistic Coulomb Hamiltonian.
- Bohr's circular orbit is most closely associated semiclassically with a circular state of maximum orbital angular momentum in a shell; it does not represent every degenerate state.
- Non-Coulomb perturbations, multi-electron interactions, fine structure, finite nuclear mass, and the Lamb shift break or modify the simple ideal result.
- The Your Turn ideal answer is `−13.6/9≈−1.51 eV`.
- No claim is made that Bohr's formula matches all measured decimal places.
