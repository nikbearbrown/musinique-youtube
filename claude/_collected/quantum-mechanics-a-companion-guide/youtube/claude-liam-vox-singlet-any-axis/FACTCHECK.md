# Fact check

VERDICT: PASS

- The spin singlet is (|↑↓⟩−|↓↑⟩)/√2 and has total angular momentum j=0.
- Under the same rotation on both particles, the singlet is invariant (an overall global phase would also be physically irrelevant).
- Same-axis measurements have outcomes (+,−) and (−,+), each with probability 1/2; (+,+) and (−,−) have probability zero.
- Each one-particle reduced state is maximally mixed: local outcomes are 50/50 and local polarization vanishes for every axis.
- Each constituent remains a spin-1/2 particle; “no individual pure spin state” is not “no spin.”
