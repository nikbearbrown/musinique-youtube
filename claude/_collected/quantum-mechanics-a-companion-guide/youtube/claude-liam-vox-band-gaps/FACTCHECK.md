# Fact check

VERDICT: PASS

- Bloch states in a periodic potential are labeled by crystal momentum/wave vector k; in the 1D Kronig–Penney model the dispersion condition has cos(ka) on the bounded side.
- If the energy-dependent right-hand side has magnitude greater than one, no real k solves the condition; those energies lie in band gaps.
- Allowed and forbidden refer to energy intervals for extended states in the ideal periodic model, not forbidden spatial regions.
- Metallic behavior can arise from a partially filled band; a filled valence band plus a large gap yields insulating behavior; semiconductors have smaller gaps.
- Real materials may have defect or surface states in nominal gaps, so the narration consistently scopes the claim to the ideal periodic model.
