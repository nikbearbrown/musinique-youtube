# Fact Check

VERDICT: PASS

- A localized free-particle packet requires a superposition of wave numbers/momenta.
- For a nonrelativistic free particle, E = ℏ²k²/(2m), so ω = ℏk²/(2m) and group velocity dω/dk = ℏk/m.
- A finite spread in k therefore produces a spread in group velocities and free-packet dispersion.
- Unitary evolution conserves total probability while a Gaussian packet broadens and its peak falls.
- The explanation distinguishes coherent dispersion from stochastic environmental diffusion.
- Exclusions honored: no Fourier-transform derivation, no explicit σ(t) formula, no uncertainty-relation derivation.
