# Fact check

VERDICT: PASS

- Quantum alternatives add as amplitudes; probability is the squared modulus of the total amplitude.
- Equal phasors reinforce at relative phase 0 and cancel at relative phase π.
- For equal slit magnitudes, intensity varies as a cosine-squared function of half the relative phase.
- A single real coordinate cannot encode an independent continuous phase in the same dimension. Complex amplitudes can be represented by pairs of real coordinates only by doubling real dimension.
- The video does not claim that real signed numbers can never cancel; it explicitly limits the fixed-dimension claim.
