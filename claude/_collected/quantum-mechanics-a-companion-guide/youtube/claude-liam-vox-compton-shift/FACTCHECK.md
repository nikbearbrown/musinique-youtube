# Fact Check

- Photon energy and momentum are `E=hc/λ` and `p=h/λ`.
- For scattering from an electron initially at rest, `Δλ = λ'−λ = (h/m_ec)(1−cosθ)`.
- The electron Compton wavelength is `h/(m_ec)=2.426310238… pm`.
- Thus `Δλ(0°)=0`, `Δλ(90°)=2.426 pm`, and `Δλ(180°)=4.853 pm`.
- At `60°`, `1−cos60°=1/2`, so the Your Turn answer is `0.5 λC≈1.213 pm`.
- Real spectra can include an unshifted component from coherent/bound-electron scattering; B02 explicitly says a second shifted peak appears alongside the original wavelength.
- “Target material does not set the scale” is scoped to the ideal free-electron kinematic shift; binding and line intensities are outside scope.
- No claim is made about the uncertain historical 2.23 pm value in the idea seed.
