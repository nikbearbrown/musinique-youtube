# Fact Check

- In the simple STM barrier model, `I(d) ∝ exp(−2κd)`.
- Therefore `I(d+Δd)/I(d) = exp(−2κΔd)`: a positive one-ångström increase divides current by `e²≈7.39` when `κ≈1 Å⁻¹`; moving closer multiplies it.
- For `φ=4 eV`, `κ=sqrt(2mφ)/ℏ≈1.025 Å⁻¹`, hence the one-ångström factor is `exp(2κ)≈7.77`, rounded to 7.7 in the example.
- The factor depends on barrier height and electronic structure; “seven” is a typical illustrative value, not a universal constant.
- A surface bump reduces the local vacuum gap at fixed tip height, producing an exponentially larger current.
- Historical and instrumentation details are kept minimal; no WKB derivation, feedback electronics, scanning modes, or surface reconstruction.
