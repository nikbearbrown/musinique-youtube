# Fact check

VERDICT: PASS

- Fermi–Dirac: n=1/(exp((E−μ)/kBT)+1), bounded by one per complete single-particle state.
- Bose–Einstein: n=1/(exp((E−μ)/kBT)−1), shown only for x=(E−μ)/kBT>0; the animation never extends through the pole into negative occupation.
- At low temperature the Fermi curve approaches a step; at high temperature/low density both approach the Maxwell–Boltzmann tail.
- Bosons can develop macroscopic ground-state occupation under appropriate condensation conditions; the narration says “can” and “under the right conditions.”
- The ± sign encodes the consequences of symmetric versus antisymmetric many-particle states; it is not offered as a derivation of spin-statistics.
