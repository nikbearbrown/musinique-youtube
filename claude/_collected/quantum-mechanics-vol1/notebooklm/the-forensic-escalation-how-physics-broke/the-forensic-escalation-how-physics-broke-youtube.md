**Title:** Why Classical Physics Failed: UV Catastrophe, Planck, and the Photon

Classical physics predicted that a warm oven should drench you in lethal ultraviolet radiation — and the math proved it, exactly. In 1900, that prediction forced physics off a cliff it couldn't climb back from.

**What you'll learn:** A NotebookLM deep dive tracing the forensic escalation: the Rayleigh–Jeans ultraviolet catastrophe, Planck's desperate quantum remedy, the three anomalies of the photoelectric effect that wave theory cannot explain, Einstein's photon, and Compton's definitive 1923 proof that light is a particle.

**The physics:** The Rayleigh–Jeans law $u(\nu,T) = (8\pi\nu^2/c^3)k_BT$ predicts infinite total energy — the ultraviolet catastrophe. Planck's fix was to restrict oscillators to discrete energies $nh\nu$, giving $u(\nu,T) = (8\pi h\nu^3/c^3)/(e^{h\nu/k_BT}-1)$ with $h = 6.626\times10^{-34}$ J·s. At ultraviolet frequencies and $T=3000$ K, the Rayleigh–Jeans prediction exceeds Planck's by a factor of $\sim 7\times10^{20}$ — twenty orders of magnitude. Einstein extended Planck's quantization to the field itself: photons carry energy $E=h\nu$, and the photoelectric equation $K_\text{max} = h\nu - \Phi$ explained the threshold, the energy–frequency dependence, and instantaneous emission, all at once.
