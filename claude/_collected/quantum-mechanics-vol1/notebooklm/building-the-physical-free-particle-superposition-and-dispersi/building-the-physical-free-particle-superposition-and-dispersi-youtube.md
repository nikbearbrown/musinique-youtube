**Title:** The Free Particle: Why a Plane Wave Fails and How a Wave Packet Saves It

The simplest quantum mechanics problem — a particle with no forces acting on it — immediately reveals a crisis: its solution can't be normalized. The fix, a wave packet, shows why quantum particles spread even in empty space.

**What you'll learn:** A NotebookLM deep dive through the plane wave non-normalizability crisis, building a Gaussian wave packet via Fourier superposition, phase velocity versus group velocity ($v_{ph} = p/2m$ vs. $v_g = p/m$), the exact spreading formula $\sigma(t) = \sigma_0\sqrt{1+(\hbar t/2m\sigma_0^2)^2}$, and why dispersal in position space is the direct mechanical consequence of the Heisenberg uncertainty principle.

**The physics:** A plane wave $\Psi_k = Ae^{i(kx-\omega t)}$ has constant $|\Psi_k|^2 = |A|^2$ everywhere — unnormalizable, implying infinite position uncertainty. A Gaussian packet of initial width $\sigma_0$ spreads as $\sigma(t) = \sigma_0\sqrt{1+(\hbar t/2m\sigma_0^2)^2}$: tight packets disperse fastest. The group velocity $v_g = \hbar k_0/m = p_0/m$ recovers classical mechanics, while the phase velocity $v_{ph} = \hbar k_0/2m$ is exactly half — the internal wave crests travel at half the speed of the probability envelope, a measurable and non-trivial prediction.
