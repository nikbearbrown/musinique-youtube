**Title:** Deriving the Infinite Square Well: Where Energy Quantization Comes From

The IBM quantum corral — 48 iron atoms arranged on a copper surface — showed standing-wave rings that nobody placed there. Confinement alone creates the quantized modes. This video shows you step-by-step exactly why.

**What you'll learn:** A NotebookLM deep dive through the full derivation: why negative energies are excluded, how the boundary condition $\psi(L)=0$ collapses a continuous set of wave vectors to a discrete ladder, the $n^2$ energy spectrum, normalization, orthonormality in three lines, and the node-counting rule that lets you read the quantum number off a graph.

**The physics:** The boundary condition $\sin(kL)=0$ forces $k_n = n\pi/L$, giving energy levels $E_n = n^2\pi^2\hbar^2/2mL^2$ — quantization from a differential equation and two walls, no postulates needed. For an electron in a 1 nm well, $E_1 \approx 0.377$ eV and the levels stand in ratios $1:4:9:16$. The normalized eigenstates $\psi_n(x) = \sqrt{2/L}\sin(n\pi x/L)$ have exactly $n-1$ interior nodes — each extra node meaning shorter wavelength, higher momentum, and higher energy.
