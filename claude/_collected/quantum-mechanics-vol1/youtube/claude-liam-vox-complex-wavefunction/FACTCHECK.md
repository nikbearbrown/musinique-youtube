# Fact Check

- `exp(ikx)` and `exp(-ikx)` both have unit probability density.
- Their phase gradients are `+k` and `-k`.
- In one dimension, `j = (hbar/m) Im(psi* dpsi/dx)`, giving opposite currents.
- `cos(kx)` is real and has zero current; it is an equal superposition of opposite momenta.
- A state may be real at a selected instant, but general Schrödinger evolution and directed flow require complex phase information.
- The video avoids the overstatement that every instantaneous wave function must have a nonzero imaginary part.
