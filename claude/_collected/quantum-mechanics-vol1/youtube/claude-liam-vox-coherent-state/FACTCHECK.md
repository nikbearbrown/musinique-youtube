# Fact Check

- Free Gaussian packets spread under quadratic free-particle dispersion.
- A harmonic-oscillator coherent state is a displaced ground state and retains the ground-state position width `sqrt(hbar/(2m omega))`.
- Its center follows the classical harmonic trajectory.
- In scaled phase space, harmonic evolution is a rigid rotation; a coherent state's circular covariance is invariant under that rotation.
- The uncertainty product remains `hbar/2`.
- Squeezed states can remain Gaussian and minimum-uncertainty while their position and momentum widths breathe; this is why “every other state distorts” was removed.
- Anharmonic evolution generally shears/deforms the packet.
- A single-mode coherent optical state is the relevant analogy; real laser output is only approximately described this way.
