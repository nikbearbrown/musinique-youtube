# Fact Check

- Each energy eigenstate has time-independent density because its time dependence is a global phase.
- A coherent two-energy superposition produces a density cross term oscillating at angular frequency `(E2-E1)/hbar`.
- For the infinite-well `n=1` and `n=2` pair, opposite parity permits an oscillating position expectation value.
- Energy measurement probabilities `|c1|^2` and `|c2|^2` remain constant, so mean energy is constant.
- A single eigenstate has no relative-phase beat.
- Coherence and spatial overlap are required for the interference term.
