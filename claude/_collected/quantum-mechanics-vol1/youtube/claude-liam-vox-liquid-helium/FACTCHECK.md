# Fact Check

- Helium-4 remains liquid down toward zero temperature at saturated vapor/low pressure and crystallizes only under pressure near 25 bar.
- Absolute zero is the ground state, not a classical state of zero motion.
- Localization implies momentum spread and nonzero kinetic energy; a harmonic mode has ground-state energy `hbar omega/2`.
- Helium's low atomic mass enhances nuclear quantum delocalization, while the helium-helium attraction is shallow.
- Pressure changes the liquid-solid free-energy balance and allows crystallization.
- The source card's “gravitational grip” was corrected to interatomic dispersion binding.
- The proposed 3 Å confinement giving about 10 meV was rejected as dimensionally/numerically unreliable.
- Superfluidity is distinct from the absence of freezing.
