# Fact Check

- For an abrupt step with `E > V1,V2`, both sides support propagating waves.
- `k_i = sqrt(2m(E-V_i))/hbar`; a downward step has larger `k` and shorter wavelength.
- Continuity of `psi` and `dpsi/dx` requires a reflected component when `k1 != k2`.
- The reflection amplitude is `(k1-k2)/(k1+k2)` and `R` is its squared magnitude.
- For `k2=2k1`, `R=1/9`.
- A sufficiently smooth spatial change suppresses reflection in the adiabatic/WKB regime.
