# Fact Check — Why Every Qubit Lives on a Globe

- A normalized pure qubit has two complex amplitudes: four real parameters.
- Normalization removes one real degree of freedom; quotienting by a common global phase removes another.
- The remaining two parameters can be written as polar and azimuthal angles on the Bloch sphere.
- North and south poles represent the computational-basis rays `|0>` and `|1>`.
- Equatorial pure states have equal basis-state probabilities, while longitude records relative phase.
- For theta=pi/2 and phi=pi/2, the Bloch vector is +y: z-basis outcomes are equiprobable and the +y outcome is certain.
- Scope correction: only pure states are on the surface. Mixed qubit states occupy the interior of the Bloch ball.

No gate operations, expectation-value derivation, or spinor double-cover discussion is used.
