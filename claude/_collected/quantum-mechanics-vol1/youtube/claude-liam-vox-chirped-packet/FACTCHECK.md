# Fact Check

- A freely evolving nonrelativistic particle has `omega(k)=hbar k^2/(2m)` and group velocity `v_g=hbar k/m`.
- Free evolution multiplies momentum amplitudes by phase factors, so the momentum probability distribution remains constant.
- A Gaussian acquires a quadratic spatial phase while its envelope broadens.
- The local wavenumber is the spatial phase gradient and has the form `k_local=k0+C(t)(x-x_c)` with positive `C(t)` for forward free evolution from an initially unchirped Gaussian.
- Position and momentum become correlated: faster/high-k components favor the leading side and slower/low-k components the trailing side.
- Tail-local wavelength is mathematically definable but has low observational weight where amplitude is negligible.
- The source card's `5.8/4.2 nm^-1` seed was removed because its value depends on width conventions and evolution parameters not fully specified.
