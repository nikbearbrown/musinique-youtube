# Fact Check — Phase and Group Velocity

- For a free nonrelativistic particle, `omega(k) = hbar k^2/(2m)`.
- A constant-phase crest moves at `v_ph = omega/k = hbar k/(2m) = p/(2m)`.
- A narrow packet envelope moves at `v_g = d omega/dk = hbar k/m = p/m`.
- Therefore `v_g = 2 v_ph`: the envelope outruns the crests.
- Relative to the right-moving envelope, crests move leftward: they enter at the leading edge and leave at the trailing edge.
- The factor two is specific to quadratic dispersion, not universal.

The source card title and its “born at the back, dying at the front” language reverse the correct relative motion. This build explicitly corrects both.
