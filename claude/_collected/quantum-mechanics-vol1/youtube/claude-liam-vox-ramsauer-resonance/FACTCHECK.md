# Fact Check

- Scope: coherent 1D scattering with `E > V0` through an ideal rectangular barrier.
- The internal wave number `q` is real and differs from the outside wave number.
- Multiple internal reflections interfere.
- For the standard rectangular barrier, `sin(qL)=0`, equivalently `qL=n*pi`, yields ideal `T=1`, `R=0`.
- This corresponds to an integer number of half wavelengths inside.
- Absorption, disorder, dephasing, finite energy width, and nonideal geometry can reduce or broaden the resonance.
