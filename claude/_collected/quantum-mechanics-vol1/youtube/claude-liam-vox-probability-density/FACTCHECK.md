# Fact Check

- In one dimension, `rho(x)=|psi(x)|^2` has units of inverse length.
- `P(x in [u,v])=integral_u^v rho(x) dx` is dimensionless and lies between zero and one for a normalized state.
- For `psi=(1/sqrt(a)) exp(-|x|/a)`, `rho=(1/a)exp(-2|x|/a)` and the whole-line integral is one.
- At `a=0.5 nm`, `rho(0)=2 nm^-1`.
- `P(|x|<=b)=1-exp(-2b/a)`; for `b=0.1 nm`, this is `1-exp(-0.4)=0.32968`.
- The source card's rough “2 nm^-1 over a 0.1 nm region gives 0.2” conflated half-width and total width; the exact symmetric interval is used instead.
- A point has zero probability for an absolutely continuous distribution, despite possibly nonzero density there.
- Density values change under unit conversion; interval probabilities do not.
