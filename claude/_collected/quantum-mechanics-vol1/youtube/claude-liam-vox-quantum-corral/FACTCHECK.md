# Fact Check

- Crommie, Lutz, and Eigler reported a circular corral of 48 Fe adatoms with radius 71.3 Å on Cu(111) in 1993.
- Cu(111) surface-state electrons form an approximately two-dimensional electron gas.
- Fe adatoms strongly scatter surface-state waves; the corral is not a mathematically perfect hard wall.
- STM/STS contrast maps local density of states at energies selected by bias, not literal particle tracks or total electron density.
- Circular confinement supports radial and angular resonance patterns; concentric rings correspond to rotationally symmetric contributions.
- The source card's 14 nm/7 nm wavelength seed incorrectly applies a one-dimensional half-wave rule to a two-dimensional disk and was removed.
