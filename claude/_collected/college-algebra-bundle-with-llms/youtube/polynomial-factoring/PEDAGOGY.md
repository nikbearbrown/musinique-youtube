# PEDAGOGY — polynomial-factoring

**Topic**: Polynomial Factoring Decision Tree  
**Level**: College Algebra  
**Audience**: Students learning to factor polynomials systematically

## Learning objective

Students will apply the factoring decision tree (GCF → term count → strategy) to factor any polynomial they encounter in college algebra, and verify their answer by expanding.

## Pedagogical approach

**Algorithm over intuition**: Trial and error requires pattern recognition built over years. A decision tree gives students a procedure on day one. The reel argues this is not a crutch — it is exactly how systematic mathematical thinking works.

**Visible branching**: The tree in B04 makes the decision points explicit: term count determines strategy. Students often skip this and jump straight to guessing factors. The visual forces the sequential logic.

**Worked trace (B06)**: Tracing x²+5x+6 through all six steps, including the failed pair (1×6), models the process of checking and rejecting options without panic. Students learn that "checking a pair and rejecting it" is part of the algorithm, not a mistake.

**Verification as last step**: The check step (expand and confirm) is explicitly included in the worked example. This normalizes verification as a required step, not optional.
