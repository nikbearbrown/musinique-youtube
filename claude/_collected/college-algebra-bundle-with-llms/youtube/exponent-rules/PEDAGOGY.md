# PEDAGOGY — exponent-rules

**Topic**: Exponent Rules — Derivation of a⁰ = 1 and All Six Rules  
**Level**: College Algebra (prerequisite review)  
**Audience**: Students entering first-semester algebra or precalculus

## Learning objective

Students will derive a⁰ = 1 from the quotient rule (not memorize it as a definition) and correctly apply all six exponent rules with numerical verification.

## Pedagogical approach

**Derivation over memorization**: The reel's central move is proving a⁰ = 1 before listing all six rules. This reframes rules as consequences of a consistent system, not arbitrary facts to memorize. Students who understand the derivation can reconstruct any rule they forget.

**Pattern extension**: The negative exponent rule is presented as a natural extension of the quotient rule below zero — the same logic, applied further. This "pattern consistency" framing is more durable than "flip to get a positive exponent."

**Gold box highlight**: The final statement "a⁰ = 1 for any a ≠ 0" appears in a gold box, signaling it as the key result of the proof. The caveat about 0⁰ is placed below in SLATE, distinguishing it from the main result.

**Numerical anchoring (B06)**: All six rules are paired with base-2 examples that students can verify by hand. This converts abstract notation into checkable arithmetic.
