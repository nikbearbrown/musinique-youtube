# PEDAGOGY — pemdas-ambiguity

**Topic**: PEMDAS / Order of Operations — Implicit Multiplication Ambiguity  
**Level**: College Algebra (prerequisite review)  
**Audience**: Students entering first-semester algebra or precalculus

## Learning objective

Students will recognize that implicit multiplication is not a formally defined precedence level and will rewrite any ambiguous expression using explicit parentheses before evaluating it.

## Pedagogical approach

**Productive conflict**: The reel opens with a genuinely controversial expression (8÷2(2+2)) that has two correct answers under different but equally valid conventions. This creates cognitive dissonance — the student cannot dismiss one answer as simply wrong. The resolution is to abandon the ambiguous expression, not choose a side.

**Two-column visualization**: Placing both interpretations side by side makes the fork in the logic visible. Students see exactly where the evaluation paths diverge (after the division sign).

**Rule table (B06)**: Grounding the fix in the PEMDAS table shows that the ambiguity arises in the M/D row, where convention says "left to right" but does not specify how juxtaposition fits. The two boxed expressions show the unambiguous forms.

**Transferable rule**: "Write the parentheses every time" is the actionable takeaway — simple, absolute, and applicable to every subsequent algebra problem.
