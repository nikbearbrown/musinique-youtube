# FACTCHECK — pemdas-ambiguity

**Topic**: Order of Operations / PEMDAS Ambiguity

## Claims verified

1. **8÷2(2+2) is genuinely ambiguous** — Correct. This viral expression has been debated by mathematicians and educators. The source of ambiguity is the status of implicit multiplication (juxtaposition). Different style guides treat it differently.

2. **Interpretation A (implicit mult. binds tighter): 8÷[2×(2+2)] = 8÷8 = 1** — Correct arithmetic. This follows the convention used in many physics and some math texts where juxtaposition has higher precedence than explicit ÷.

3. **Interpretation B (strict left-to-right after same precedence): (8÷2)×(2+2) = 4×4 = 16** — Correct arithmetic. This follows PEMDAS/BODMAS as taught in most US K-12 and college algebra courses, where × and ÷ are evaluated left to right.

4. **PEMDAS: Parentheses, Exponents, Multiplication/Division, Addition/Subtraction** — Standard mnemonic, correct.

5. **Multiplication and division are the same precedence level, evaluated left to right** — Correct per standard PEMDAS convention.

6. **(8÷2)(2+2) = 4×4 = 16** — Unambiguous; correct.

7. **8÷(2(2+2)) = 8÷8 = 1** — Unambiguous; correct.

8. **Implicit multiplication is not a formal precedence level in most style guides** — Accurate. AMS, ISO, and most textbooks do not assign juxtaposition a distinct precedence; they recommend explicit parentheses.

## No mathematical errors found. The ambiguity claim is genuine and well-documented.
