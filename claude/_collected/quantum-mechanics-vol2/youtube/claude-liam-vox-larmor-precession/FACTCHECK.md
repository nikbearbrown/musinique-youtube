# Fact Check

- For a spin in a static z-directed magnetic field, the two z-basis amplitudes retain their magnitudes while their relative phase advances.
- The Bloch vector therefore precesses at constant polar angle; `S_z` expectation and the up/down probabilities stay constant.
- The angular Larmor frequency magnitude is `|omega|=|gamma|B`; the sign fixes the direction under a chosen convention.
- Proton `gamma/(2 pi)` is approximately 42.577 MHz/T, giving about 63.9 MHz at 1.5 T and 127.7 MHz at 3 T.
- For a pure spin-half state tilted by `theta`, `<S_z>=(hbar/2)cos(theta)`; at 60 degrees this is `hbar/4`.
- A static field alone does not cause alignment. Relaxation and dephasing involve coupling to the environment and ensemble effects, beyond this explainer.
