# Fact Check

- The angular-momentum commutation algebra admits integer and half-integer irreducible representations.
- Scalar orbital wavefunctions on ordinary space impose the integer azimuthal condition and therefore integer orbital `ell`.
- Intrinsic spin uses spinor representations; a spinor changes sign under a `2pi` rotation but the state ray is unchanged.
- Correction: particles with spin can have spinor-valued position-space wavefunctions. Spin is internal rather than nonexistent in position space.
- A global sign is unobservable by itself, while relative phase can be detected interferometrically.
