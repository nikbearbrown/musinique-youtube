# Fact Check

- Ehrenfest gives `d<x>/dt=<p>/m` and `d<p>/dt=<F(x)>` exactly for the standard Hamiltonian.
- Classical closure requires `<F(x)> approx F(<x>)`, exact for at most linear force and approximate for a narrow packet in a smooth potential.
- In an even potential, `F(x)` is odd; a symmetric probability density gives both `<x>=0` and `<F>=0`.
- The expectation value need not be a mode or likely single-shot outcome.
- Equal narrow lobes near `x=+/-2` have mean approximately zero and variance approximately four when their internal widths are negligible.
