# Fact Check

- Hydrogen's ground-state hyperfine rest frequency is about 1420.4058 MHz, corresponding to 21.106 cm and 5.874 micro-eV.
- Coupling electron and proton spin-half gives an `F=1` triplet and `F=0` singlet.
- The `m_F=0` triplet is the symmetric plus combination; the singlet is the antisymmetric minus combination.
- The spin-dot-spin eigenvalues are `+hbar^2/4` for `F=1` and `-3hbar^2/4` for `F=0`; hydrogen's triplet is higher.
- The line is observed in emission and absorption, and Doppler shifts reveal line-of-sight velocity.
- A 100 kHz redshift corresponds to about 21.1 km/s recession at nonrelativistic speed.
