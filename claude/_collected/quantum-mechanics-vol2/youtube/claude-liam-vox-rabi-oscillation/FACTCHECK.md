# Fact Check

- Under ideal coherent resonant driving, a two-level system can undergo complete population inversion.
- In the rotating-frame picture on resonance, the state rotates about the transverse drive axis; drive amplitude sets the Rabi rate.
- A pi-pulse lasts half a Rabi cycle. For `f_R=20 kHz`, `t_pi=1/(2f_R)=25 microseconds`.
- Detuning changes the rotation axis and limits maximum transfer; decoherence and inhomogeneity also reduce inversion.
- The dressed-state/quasienergy splitting gives the resonant Rabi angular frequency when divided by `hbar`, within the rotating-wave idealization used here.
