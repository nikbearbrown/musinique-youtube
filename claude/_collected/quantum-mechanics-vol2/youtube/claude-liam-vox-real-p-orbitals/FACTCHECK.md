# Fact Check

- The `l=1` subspace has `m=-1,0,+1`; every superposition remains an `L^2` eigenstate with eigenvalue `2 hbar^2`.
- Complex `m=+/-1` states have equal angular probability density and opposite azimuthal phase winding.
- Real `p_x` and `p_y` functions are unitary linear combinations of `m=+1` and `m=-1` states, up to convention-dependent phases.
- In `p_x` or `p_y`, measuring `L_z` gives `+hbar` and `-hbar` with probability one half; mean zero and variance `hbar^2`.
- Lobe signs are wavefunction phase, not electric charge.
- In many-electron chemistry, orbitals are generally model-dependent one-particle functions, not exact many-body wavefunctions.
