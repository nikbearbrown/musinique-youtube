# Fact Check

- Unitary evolution preserves inner products and state norms.
- A strongly continuous one-parameter unitary group has a self-adjoint generator; this is Stone's theorem in the appropriate mathematical setting.
- For time-independent `H`, `U(t)=exp(-iHt/hbar)` differentiates to the Schrödinger equation.
- Correction: probability conservation alone does not uniquely derive the Schrödinger equation or identify the physical Hamiltonian. Linearity, continuity/group structure, closed-system assumptions, and generator identification are additional inputs.
- For time-dependent Hamiltonians, a simple exponential is generally replaced by a time-ordered propagator.
- Self-adjointness, rather than merely finite-dimensional “Hermitian,” is the relevant infinite-dimensional condition.
