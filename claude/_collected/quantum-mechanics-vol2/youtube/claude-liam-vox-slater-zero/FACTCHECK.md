# Fact Check

- Pauli formulated exclusion in 1925; the 1945 Nobel citation explicitly recognized the exclusion principle.
- Slater introduced determinant wave functions in 1929.
- A Slater determinant enforces antisymmetry under particle exchange, but fermionic antisymmetry is the physical input; determinant algebra does not derive spin-statistics from nothing.
- Duplicate complete spin-orbitals give identical determinant columns and a zero state.
- Two electrons may share one spatial orbital if their spin-orbitals differ, as in helium `1s alpha` and `1s beta`.
- Shell structure also depends on the Hamiltonian and interactions, not exclusion alone.
