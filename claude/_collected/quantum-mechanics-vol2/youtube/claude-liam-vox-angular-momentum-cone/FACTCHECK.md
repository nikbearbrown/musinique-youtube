# Fact Check

- In a simultaneous eigenstate of `L^2` and `L_z`, the magnitude is `hbar sqrt(l(l+1))` and the largest allowed z projection is `l hbar`.
- Their ratio gives the conventional vector-model cone angle: `cos(theta)=l/sqrt(l(l+1))=sqrt(l/(l+1))`.
- For `l=2`, `theta=35.264 degrees`; for `l=10`, `theta=17.548 degrees`.
- In the top state `|l,l>`, `Delta L_x^2 + Delta L_y^2 = l hbar^2`; the transverse rms magnitude is therefore `hbar sqrt(l)`.
- The cone is a semiclassical/vector-model visualization of the simultaneous `L^2,L_z` eigenvalues, not a literal hidden arrow trajectory or a claim that all three components possess sharp values.
- As `l` grows, the cone angle scales approximately as `1/sqrt(l)` and the relative transverse contribution vanishes, giving the classical limit.
