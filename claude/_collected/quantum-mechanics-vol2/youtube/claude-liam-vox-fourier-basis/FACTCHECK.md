# Fact Check

- Position and momentum wavefunctions are components of the same abstract state in different bases.
- With a standard convention, they are related by a unitary Fourier transform.
- A basis change does not itself evolve or physically disturb the state.
- For a minimum-uncertainty Gaussian, `sigma_x sigma_p=hbar/2`; halving one width doubles the other.
- With `sigma_x=1 nm`, `sigma_p,min=hbar/(2 sigma_x)=5.27e-26 kg m/s`.
- Phase matters: equal position densities do not imply equal momentum distributions.
- “Turning your head” is only a finite-dimensional geometric metaphor for representation change, not a literal spatial rotation.
