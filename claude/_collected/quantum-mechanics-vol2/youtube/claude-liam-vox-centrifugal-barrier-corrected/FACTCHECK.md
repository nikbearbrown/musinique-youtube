# Fact Check

- The separated kinetic operator contains the angular term `L^2/(2 mu r^2)`.
- On a fixed-`ell` eigenstate it becomes `hbar^2 ell(ell+1)/(2 mu r^2)` and is conventionally included in the radial effective potential.
- Correction: calling it “not a potential” is too strong. It is an effective-potential contribution with kinetic origin, not a fundamental interaction potential.
- Correction: increasing `ell` suppresses small-`r` behavior, but does not alone imply a universal increase in mean orbital radius; `n` and the full potential matter.
- No new fundamental force is introduced by separation of variables.
