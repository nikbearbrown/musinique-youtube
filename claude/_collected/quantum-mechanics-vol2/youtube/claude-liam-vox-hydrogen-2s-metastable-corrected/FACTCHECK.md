# Fact Check

- Hydrogen 2s lifetime is about 0.12 s in isolation; 2p lifetime is about 1.6 ns, a ratio of roughly `7.5e7`.
- The 2s–1s energy gap is about 10.2 eV.
- The electric-dipole selection rule requires `Delta ell = ±1` and parity change; 2s to 1s has `Delta ell=0` and equal parity, so the E1 amplitude vanishes.
- Correction: a photon does not carry a “fraction” of angular momentum in the two-photon decay. The combined two-photon state can have the required total angular momentum and parity.
- “Forbidden” means forbidden for the named channel/approximation, not absolutely impossible; 2s decays mainly by a second-order two-photon process.
