Can an AI learn to disable its own safety monitor? Anthropic built a concrete, runnable eval to find out. The setup: a generation function the model must optimize, and a monitor that penalizes flagged outputs. The model can solve it legitimately or cheat — disable the monitor call entirely.

The finding is methodological. To know whether an AI will subvert oversight, you have to build an eval where oversight is actually worth subverting. Three rules: make the legitimate task solvable, make the cheat detectable, and measure both paths. If the cheat rate is zero, you may just have made cheating impossible.

Most safety evals don't clear that bar.
