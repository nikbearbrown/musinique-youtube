# PEDAGOGY — claude-liam-cap-04-build-the-deck

## Act structure
8 beats (includes B04). Cold open (B00) reframes the deck as the vehicle for understanding, not decoration, and names the exact constraint: five sections, ten minutes, each with a job. B01 covers Brand Intro (1 min): positioning first, no backstory. B02 covers Strategy + Visual Identity (2+2 min): presented vs recited, and show-don't-read. B03 covers Brand Storytelling (2 min): one story told in full, named framework with fit explanation, pillar connection. B04 covers Tool Demo (3 min): framing slide, value-ordered features, live-plus-fallback. BVDT delivers all five sections with budgets and killer mistakes. BHTF hands off the madison-pitch-style deck builder prompt — student pastes the full brand system; Claude generates slide-by-slide outlines to exact budgets. BOUT closes.

## Cold open law
Opens on the reframe: presentation quality is a separate skill from brand quality, and bad presentation costs points. Names the organizing constraint (five sections, exact time budget) before describing any section. Sets up the killer-mistake structure that each body beat will close with.

## Gap formula
Gap: students have the brand system but may build the deck by feel rather than to a section-budget discipline, leading to overruns or empty filler. Bridge: named section budgets + per-section content requirements + killer-mistake identification. Close: student runs the BHTF prompt and gets a slide-by-slide outline that matches the budgets before touching slide software.

## Utility lint
Every section has a specific time budget, a named content requirement, and exactly one killer mistake. The killer mistakes are concrete behaviors, not vague warnings: "starting with backstory" (B01), "reading the style guide aloud" (B02), "presenting a summary instead of the story" (B03), ">30 sec on a feature not demonstrating value" (B04). The BHTF prompt requests the story in full — not a summary — ensuring the student has the real material before generating the outline.

## Vocabulary / register
"Live means live" — callback to E2, applied to the demo section; reinforces the series through-line. "Framing slide" — the specific tool-demo opening instrument; named and described concretely. "Value order" — the principle for feature sequencing: obvious value first, methodology visible second. "Section-budget discipline" — the organizing frame for the deck-building episode.

## Honesty
The section budgets (1+2+2+2+3 = 10) match the course brief. The "target 9:30" guidance is consistent with E1. The "framing slide before live demo" instruction is accurate professional practice. The killer-mistake descriptions are observable behaviors, not editorial opinions.

## Length law
8 beats (~344s estimated — the longest episode). B04 is fully earned: the Tool Demo section is the largest single time budget (3 min) and has the most distinct components (framing slide, feature order, fallback). The extra length is justified because this is the deck-building tutorial episode — the most instructional in the series.

BHTF reads the full deck-builder prompt aloud with every slot named, closes with the specific action: "build the slides to match the outline — do not improvise the structure during the rehearsal."
