Role prompting is misunderstood as costume. A role prompt does not make Claude pretend. It sets register: vocabulary, assumed knowledge level, tone, and depth of explanation. That is calibration, not theater.

Role and persona are different. Role is functional calibration — "you are an expert tax attorney" sets register. Persona adds character and voice — "you are Marcus, a sardonic 1970s tax attorney who uses nautical metaphors." Both are valid. Know which you need.

The test: does the role change what counts as a good answer? If yes, use it. If the task is register-agnostic, role adds no signal.
