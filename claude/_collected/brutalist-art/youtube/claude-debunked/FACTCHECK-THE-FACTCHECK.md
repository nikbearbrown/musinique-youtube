# Fact-checking the fact-check — the "Anthropic Claude Agentic AI Ecosystem" wheel

Verified against current official sources, July 14, 2026. Verdict up front: **the
fact-check's overall read is right (the wheel mixes real names with invented
filler), but it overcorrects — two products it calls "invented" are real,
shipping, branded Anthropic products, and its Mythos critique is half wrong.**

## Where the fact-check is confirmed

**Model lineup.** Official docs list the current models as **Claude Fable 5,
Opus 4.8, Sonnet 5, Haiku 4.5**, with **Mythos 5** limited to approved customers
(Project Glasswing) and **Mythos Preview** invitation-only for defensive
cybersecurity workflows ([Claude Platform docs](https://platform.claude.com/docs/en/about-claude/models/overview)).
So yes: the infographic's "Sonnet 4.6" is outdated, and — the bigger miss both
the wheel AND the fact-check underplay — **the wheel omits Fable 5 entirely**,
the flagship, in the same month it was the biggest AI story going.

**The export-control saga.** Confirmed real: Fable 5 and Mythos 5 were suspended
under Commerce Department export controls and restored after ~19 days when the
controls were lifted June 30, 2026 ([Anthropic](https://www.anthropic.com/news/redeploying-fable-5),
[CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/30/anthropic-says-trump-admin-has-lifted-export-controls-on-claude-fable-5-and-mythos-5.html),
[Al Jazeera](https://www.aljazeera.com/economy/2026/7/1/us-lifts-restrictions-on-powerful-ai-models-fable-mythos-anthropic-says)).

**Claude Design.** Real, and the fact-check's correction is exactly right: it's
an **Anthropic Labs research preview powered by Claude Opus 4.7** (the
vision-focused model), not Opus 4.8 as the wheel claims
([Anthropic](https://www.anthropic.com/news/claude-design-anthropic-labs),
[VentureBeat](https://venturebeat.com/technology/anthropic-just-launched-claude-design-an-ai-tool-that-turns-prompts-into-prototypes-and-challenges-figma)).

**Agent SDK, Managed Agents, Claude Code, Cowork, MCP, workspace integrations.**
All real, as the fact-check says.

**The "invented infra" pattern.** Spot-checked: "Claude Context Store" and
"Safe Artifact Repo" return nothing as Anthropic product names — artifacts and
memory exist as *features*, not as those branded products. Constitutional AI is
a training method, not a shippable "checker." The perfectly symmetrical wheel
with four boxes per spoke remains the tell.

## Where the fact-check is WRONG

**Claude Marketplace is real.** It sits on the fact-check's "invented" list —
but [claude.com/platform/marketplace](https://claude.com/platform/marketplace)
exists, and the launch was covered as enterprise access to Claude-powered tools
from Replit, GitLab, Harvey and others
([VentureBeat](https://venturebeat.com/technology/anthropic-launches-claude-marketplace-giving-enterprises-access-to-claude),
[InfoWorld](https://www.infoworld.com/article/4142340/anthropic-debuts-claude-marketplace-to-target-ai-procurement-bottlenecks.html)).
The wheel's one-line description is roughly accurate.

**Claude Security is real.** Also on the "invented" list — but
[anthropic.com/product/security](https://www.anthropic.com/product/security)
exists; it launched in beta and rolled out to Enterprise customers
([SecurityWeek](https://www.securityweek.com/anthropic-unveils-claude-security-to-counter-ai-powered-exploit-surge/),
[Techzine](https://www.techzine.eu/news/security/140944/anthropic-claude-security-available-to-all-enterprise-customers/)).
The wheel's "Zero Trust framework" phrasing may embellish the positioning, but
the branded product exists.

**The Mythos critique is half wrong.** The fact-check calls the wheel's Mythos
description "stale/wrong" — but current official docs say Mythos Preview is
invitation-only for defensive cybersecurity workflows, which is more or less
"restricted to select security orgs." The genuinely unsupported part of the
wheel's claim is "public rollout confirmed in coming weeks" — Mythos 5 remains
limited-availability, and nothing announces a public rollout.

## The lesson (and yes, it's an episode)

The fact-check commits the mirror image of the infographic's sin. The wheel
invents products to complete a pattern; the fact-check *un-invents* products to
complete a debunking. Both fail the same way: pattern-confidence outrunning
verification. "Claude Marketplace" sounds like template filler — so the checker
filed it as filler without looking. The rule the claude-explainer series should
say out loud: **a fact-check that doesn't cite sources is just a second opinion.**
This is "Claude, Oversimplified" all over again with a better costume —
candidate title: **"Claude, Debunked?"** — grading the debunkers.
