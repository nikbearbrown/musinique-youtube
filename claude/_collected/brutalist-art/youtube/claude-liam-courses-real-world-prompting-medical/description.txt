Medical prompting is not harder than general prompting. It's just more consequential. Four guardrails cover almost every failure mode: role, uncertainty language, escalation instruction, and a standing disclaimer.

Role is calibration, not decoration — it sets the clinical register and the assumed knowledge level. Uncertainty language prevents false confidence. An escalation trigger tells Claude when to stop and refer. The disclaimer sets scope.

Bake all four into the system prompt once. Applied every turn. Strip any one of them and the failure mode becomes visible in the output.
