You cannot stop Claude from hallucinating by asking nicely. The reliable method is grounding: provide the source document in the prompt, tell Claude to answer only from that document, and instruct it to say "I don't know" if the answer isn't there.

The next level: tell Claude to cite the exact passage it draws from, then verify it appears verbatim in the source. A citation that doesn't appear in the source is a hallucination you can detect. The verifiable citation is the audit trail.

Confidence and correctness are uncorrelated in a next-token predictor. Grounding is the mechanism that connects them.
