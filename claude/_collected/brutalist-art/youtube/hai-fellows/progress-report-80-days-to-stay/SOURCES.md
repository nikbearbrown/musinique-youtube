## Statistics cited on-screen (all trace to this article, none invented)

- The “80 Days to Stay” initiative is a data-driven effort by Humanitarians AI to address a critical information gap facing international students and skilled workers on OPT and H-1B visas in the United States.
- Research from NAFSA, Interstride, and IZA indicates that only 44.6% of international students secure employment after graduation, compared to 62.1% of domestic students.
- International students submit roughly twice as many job applications (45 versus 22 on average) but receive 30% fewer offers and land full-time roles at a rate of only 38.1%, compared to 53.1% for domestic graduates.
- The irony is striking: students on F-1 OPT status require no employer sponsorship at all, and employers who hire them are actually exempt from FICA payroll taxes (a savings of approximately 7.65%), yet roughly 75% of employers do not understand OPT or CPT work authorization rules.
- The “80 Days to Stay” project exists to close this gap.
- The project’s success criterion is concrete and human: facilitate at least one successful employment match that preserves a person’s visa status within 80 days.
- Of the 30,370 total records, 27,876 companies (91.8%) carry funding data from SEC filings.
- The remaining 2,494 records (8.2%) were likely sourced from the DOL or USCIS datasets where a corresponding SEC filing could not be matched, meaning these are employers with known visa sponsorship activity but without a confirmed private fundraising event in the SEC data.
