**B01 · STILL·archive·kenburns** — the quantum corral (1993, IBM)
> In 1993, IBM scientists arranged forty-eight iron atoms into a ring, then
> photographed the space inside. Concentric ripples. Nobody drew them —
> confinement did.

**B02 · CARD** — THE INFINITE SQUARE WELL / why boxes make whole numbers
> Last film: only waves that fit survive. This film: trap a particle in a
> box, and count the notes it's allowed to play.

**B03 · GRAPHIC** — guitar string, and where the analogy breaks
> A guitar string pinned at both ends plays only whole humps — one, two,
> three. Quantum swaps the string for probability itself. Same walls.
> Different thing waving.

**B04 · GRAPHIC** — the derivation lands on one line
> Schrödinger's equation in the box allows any energy — until the walls
> speak. The wave must die at both ends: sine of k L equals zero. Whole
> numbers only.

**T01–T03 · equation tangent — E_n = n²π²ℏ²/2mL²** (zones 2→3→4; spotlights)
> T01: The energy menu in one line: n squared times a floor price, set by
> the particle's mass and the width of its box. Nothing in between.
> T02: n counts the humps: one, two, three. h-bar makes it quantum. Heavier
> particle or wider box — every note in the menu drops.
> T03: An electron in a one-nanometer box: level one costs point three-eight
> electron volts — chemistry's scale. A marble in a centimeter box: ten to
> the minus forty-two. Invisible.

**B05 · COMPOSITE** — Bohr 1913 vs Schrödinger 1926 (archive portraits plate)
> In 1913, Bohr decreed quantization — a postulate. In 1926, Schrödinger
> derived it from a differential equation and two boundary conditions.
> Nature's whole numbers, explained.

**B06 · GRAPHIC** — the n² ladder and the nodes
> The levels climb as n squared — one, four, nine, sixteen. Each higher
> note adds one more zero-crossing: shorter wave, faster particle, higher
> rung.

**B07 · GRAPHIC** — zero-point energy (the bottom rung is never zero)
> And the bottom rung is never zero. A trapped particle can't sit still —
> stillness plus confinement violates the uncertainty principle. Being
> boxed in costs energy.

**B08 · GRAPHIC** — the femtosecond slosh
> Mix the two lowest notes and the probability sloshes across the box every
> three point seven femtoseconds. Motion is the beat between quantum notes.

**B09 · STILL·archive** — the corral, again (full circle)
> Those rings at IBM are this exact mathematics, photographed — standing
> waves of probability, penned by forty-eight atoms. The walls impose the
> notes. Nature obeys.

**B10 · CARD** — next: FINITE WELLS & BARRIERS (ch 6)
> Next: real walls are never infinitely tall. Soften them, and particles
> begin to leak straight through. Tunneling.
