You cannot ask a model if it is politically biased and trust the answer. You have to measure it.

The method is paired prompts: ask the same structural question about politically opposite subjects, then compare the outputs. A biased model is longer on one side, refuses one subject more often, or hedges more in one direction. None of these are invisible — all three produce numeric outputs.

Three rules for a valid eval: structural identity in the pair, many pairs across many topics, and measurement of output quality not just refusal rate. Engagement alone is not even-handedness.
