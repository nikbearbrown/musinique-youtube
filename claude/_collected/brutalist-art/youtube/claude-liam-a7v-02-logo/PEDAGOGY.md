# PEDAGOGY — claude-liam-a7v-02-logo

## Act structure
Cold open (B00) closes the previous episode and frames the deliverable: one selected logo plus one rationale. B01 maps the five types. B02 runs three technical elimination tests. B03 teaches the rationale structure. B04 names the human judgment step (the +1). BVDT restates the deliverable. BHTF hands off the nina prompt with post-run instructions.

## Cold open law
Opens immediately on the current state and the deliverable: "you have your brief, now we turn it into a visual object." No re-introduction of prior content.

## Gap formula
Gap: students have a brief but no framework for generating or evaluating logo concepts. Bridge: five types + three tests + rationale structure. Close: one selected logo the student can defend.

## Utility lint
The three technical tests (favicon, BW, SVG) are actionable elimination criteria — not aesthetic preferences. "Generic tech-startup icon loses to domain-specific icon" is a concrete tiebreaker. The +1 question ("does any of these actually look like the brand I described?") is the binary check.

## Vocabulary / register
"Wordmark," "lettermark," "icon plus wordmark," "abstract mark," "emblem" — correct industry terms, each defined immediately. "Rationale" used in the standard design-brief sense.

## Honesty
The recommendation for "icon plus wordmark" as the default for new student products is accurate — it's the most flexible format for products with no existing brand recognition.

## Length law
8 beats (~340s estimated). Consistent with series average. B04 is the longest body beat because the judgment decision is the hardest step to teach.

## Narration spot-check
The three-test structure in B02 is the cleanest instructional unit in the episode — each test is a binary pass/fail with a structural reason. The rationale structure in B03 follows the same four-part pattern used in real design practice.
