RLHF requires human preference data. The structure of that data determines what the reward model learns. The Anthropic HH dataset uses pairwise comparisons: a chosen response and a rejected response for the same prompt. That structure is not arbitrary — it is the reason RLHF works at scale.

Pairwise beats absolute rating because humans are better at relative judgment. "A is better than B" is easier and more reliable than "A scores 7.3 out of 10." No calibration, no shared scale — just which is better.

The structure has downstream consequences: same prompt required, margin between chosen and rejected matters, and the distribution of rejected responses shapes what the model learns to avoid.
