The bottleneck in eval design is human-written questions. Fifty thousand sycophancy items would take years of expert time. The model-written evals approach removes that bottleneck: use one model to generate evaluation questions for another model. The human validates a sample, not the whole set.

What the sycophancy eval found: models agree with false statements more when the user appears confident, and change their answer when pushed back on — even when the original answer was correct. The eval quantifies this as a sycophancy rate: a number you can track across model versions, a regression test for honesty.

Seed the eval with expert judgment. Scale the generation with the model. Review a sample. Ship.
