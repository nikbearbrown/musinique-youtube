**B03 · STILL·kenburns** — Schrödinger, 1926 (PORTRAIT prompt)

**B04 · GRAPHIC** — the curves from our simulation (blue hump, orange ripple)

**B05 · DOCUMENT·annotate** — Born's paper, the footnote (THE PLANE BEAT)
> Born's draft said the probability of finding the particle is proportional
> to psi. The footnote fixed it: not psi — psi squared. One symbol, added in
> proof.

**T01–T03 · equation tangent — P = ∫ₐᵇ|ψ|²dx** (zones 2→3→4; word-keyed spotlights)
> T01: Born's rule is one line: the probability of finding the particle in a
> region is the integral of psi squared over that region. Odds, not certainty.
> T02: Psi is the wave function. Psi squared is a density — probability per
> nanometer, like population per square mile. The integral adds it up over
> your region.
> T03: Take psi as a falling exponential. Within one width of center:
> eighty-six percent. At the peak the density reads two per nanometer —
> bigger than one, perfectly legal.

**B06 · GRAPHIC** — two packets, identical humps, opposite motion
> Here's the strange part: psi is complex. Two packets, identical blue humps.
> One moves left, one moves right. The hump can't tell you which. The phase
> can.

**B07 · GRAPHIC** — the i is structural (imaginary part regenerates)
> That's not decoration. Schrödinger's equation has an i built in — erase the
> imaginary part and take one time-step: it grows right back. Motion lives in
> the phase.

**B08 · GRAPHIC** — what spreads is the histogram (dots echo of film three)
> And when the blue hump spreads, the particle is not smearing out. Detect it:
> one dot. Repeat the experiment thousands of times — the dots build psi
> squared.

**B09 · COMPOSITE** — the moral (ai plate + chips)
> The wave function is not the particle. It is the rule for the odds. Every
> histogram ever measured agrees with the footnote.

**B10 · STILL·kenburns** — Born kicker (PORTRAIT prompt, direct gaze)
> Who was Max Born? When Einstein wrote that God does not play dice, the
> letter was addressed to Born. His Nobel came twenty-eight years after the
> footnote.
