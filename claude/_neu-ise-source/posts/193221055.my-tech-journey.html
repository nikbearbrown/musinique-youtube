I didn’t plan a straight path into tech. I followed the work, said yes to problems that scared me a little, and figured things out along the way.

LTIMindtree - Starting with the Basics

My first role was as a Software Engineer at LTIMindtree, working on a Citi Bank engagement. The job was a mix of Manual and Automation Testing - collaborating with BAs and Developers, reporting bugs, and building Selenium automation scripts for repeated test scenarios. The project had many interlinked modules, so cross-functional knowledge wasn’t optional - it was survival.

Early lesson: The most valuable work isn’t always glamorous. Sometimes it’s the script that saves your team two hours every morning.

Johnson & Johnson - Building from the Ground Up

This one changed my trajectory. I joined a Cloud Engineering project for J&J on Azure as the sole engineer alongside my Manager. Two larger vendors - Accenture and Hanu - were already there with bigger teams. Rather than competing directly, I focused on building relationships, sharing knowledge, and earning trust through quality delivery.

For each Azure service, I designed the full architecture - Service Design, Encryption, Logging, Enforcement, and Cost Optimization - using Custom Policies and Function App runbooks, tested across four environments with Bitbucket, Git, and Jenkins. Every design went through J&J’s Information Security Risk Management team before going live.

The client expanded our scope. My team grew from just me to seven engineers. I trained juniors, managed cross-vendor coordination, and handled client requirements - leading to my promotion as Senior Cloud Engineer. J&J and Microsoft both recognized LTIMindtree as a Trusted Cloud Partner, opening the door to new projects.

In parallel, I led a cloud security initiative using Cloud Conformity. We improved the secure score from 68% to 96%, eliminated unused resources, and reduced cloud costs significantly.

Fidelity Investments - Seeing Tech from the Other Side

I moved to the US and joined Fidelity as an IT Audit Analyst Co-op - a deliberate shift to broaden my thinking beyond pure engineering.

I worked across five internal audits covering Cloud (AWS and Azure), API Security, IT Security Management, and GRC tools like TeamMate+ and Archer. I used SQL on Snowflake for audit testing and performed database user access reviews. But the real learning was bigger: understanding business processes and auditing them from a risk perspective - identifying control gaps, presenting findings to leadership, and drafting remediation plans.

What I Carry Forward

Every role taught me something the last one couldn’t. Testing taught me rigor. Cloud engineering taught me ownership. Auditing taught me how to see systems from the outside in.

The journey isn’t over - but the foundation is something I’m proud of.
